Why Marine Intelligence Weekly Exists

Maritime AI is developing faster than most professionals can track it. Classification societies are issuing new evidence standards. The IMO is building regulatory frameworks for autonomous ships. Indian companies are deploying AI at port scale. Global companies are raising billions and setting benchmarks that every fleet will operate within.

Marine Intelligence Weekly exists because the maritime professional — whether on the bridge, in the engine room, or managing a fleet from a Mumbai office — deserves analysis that is verified, operationally relevant, and written by someone who understands what happens below the waterline.

Every issue is written from the engine room perspective. No vendor relationships. No advertising. No marketing language. Just verified intelligence from primary sources — IMO, classification societies, and operational deployments.

Coverage Areas

What Marine Intelligence Weekly Covers

Every issue tracks the signals that matter for maritime professionals — from IMO committee outcomes to classification society rule changes, from autonomous vessel deployments to decarbonisation compliance deadlines.

Regulation

SOLAS amendments, MARPOL Annex VI, STCW updates, Load Line Convention, MLC 2006, ISM Code, ISPS — tracked from IMO committee sessions through entry into force, with examiner-level detail for MEO Class 1 preparation.

Decarbonisation

CII ratings, EEXI compliance, EEDI, FuelEU Maritime, IMO 2023 GHG Strategy, alternative fuels (hydrogen, ammonia, methanol, LNG), DNV-RP-0703, and the full regulatory lineage from UNFCCC to fleet operations.

Maritime AI & MASS

IMO MASS Code, autonomous vessel deployments, AI navigation systems, LR and DNV class notations for autonomous ships, IACS UR E26, Remote Operation Centres, and the operational technology signals that bridge and engine room teams need to track.

Classification Societies

Rule changes from Lloyd's Register, DNV, ABS, Bureau Veritas, ClassNK, and IACS — translated from circular language into what they mean for your next survey, vetting inspection, and PSC examination.

MEO Class 1 Exam

Oral exam questions with regulation-referenced answers, covering SOLAS chapters, MARPOL annexes, STCW competence tables, ISM audits, and the latest IMO instruments. Every issue includes an Oral Exam Corner written from the examiner's perspective.

India & Asia Maritime

Indian maritime AI ecosystem, DG Shipping circulars, smart port deployments at major Indian ports, and the Asia-Pacific autonomous vessel and decarbonisation developments that affect Indian seafarers and fleet managers.
