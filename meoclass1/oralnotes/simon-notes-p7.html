CO₂ System — Properties, Pressure Testing & Measurement Methods (Pages 151–152)
FireSOLAS

Physical Properties of CO₂

  • Critical Temperature: 31.1°C — above this, CO₂ cannot be liquefied regardless of pressure.
  • Critical Pressure: 75 bar — pressure needed to liquefy at critical temperature.
  • Bottle pressure: 56 bar at 20°C; 64 bar at 25°C; 71 bar at 30°C — room temperature must be kept below 55°C.
  • Specific free gas volume: 0.56 m³/kg at 21.1°C and 1 bar.

Pressure Testing of CO₂ Flooding System Lines

Line SectionTest MediumTest Pressure
Cylinder to Master ValveLiquid (hydraulic)190 bar
Master Valve to ER/Cargo Hold ValveLiquid (hydraulic)80 bar
ER/Cargo Hold Valve to NozzlesAir blowing test6–7 bar
Pilot Control Line (copper tube)Burst test56 bar

CO₂ Bottle Stamping Requirements

  • Manufacturer name, official stamp, serial number, tare weight (empty bottle weight), test pressure, and hydrostatic test date.

CO₂ Quantity Measurement Methods

  • Beam scale (physical weighing).
  • Thermal strip indicators.
  • Infrared (IR) camera.
  • Ultrasonic liquid level indicator.
  • Radioactive isotope liquid level gauge.
  • Hydrostatic pressure testing history.

Capacity Requirements

  • Machinery space (excl. casing): CO₂ = 40% of gross volume.
  • Machinery space (incl. casing): CO₂ = 35% of gross volume.
  • Cargo hold: CO₂ = 30% of gross volume of largest cargo hold.
  • Discharge requirement: 50% within 1 minute; 85% within 2 minutes.
⚖ Regulatory References

Simon Sir typically asks: "What pressure is the CO₂ line tested to?" — Give all three sections: 190 bar (cylinder to master valve), 80 bar (master valve to ER valve), 6–7 bar air blow (nozzle section). He will ask about capacity — know 40%/35%/30% by heart. Pilot line is copper — he may ask why (corrosion resistance, flexibility, ease of installation).

CO₂ Room Regulations, Maintenance Schedule & Certificates (Pages 146–150)
FireSOLAS

CO₂ Room Regulations (FSS Code Ch. 5)

  • Must not be used for any other purpose; located aft of the collision bulkhead.
  • Access doors must open outward; room must be gas-tight.
  • Access directly possible from the open deck.
  • Room temperature must be maintained below 55°C.
  • Exhaust duct of mechanical ventilation routed to the bottom of the space (CO₂ is 1.5× heavier than air).
  • Minimum 6 air changes per hour; minimum clear height: 2,000 mm.
  • Must not be located lower than one deck below the open deck if below deck.

CO₂ System Maintenance Schedule

IntervalKey Tasks
MonthlyOpen control cabinet — test audible/visual alarms, ventilation cut-off. Check room ventilation fan. Verify cylinders properly clamped. Check operating levers and flexible hoses.
YearlyAll monthly checks + blow through distribution pipes with service air + check general physical condition of installation.
2-Yearly (Biennial)All yearly checks + weigh CO₂ bottles (max 10% content reduction per cylinder allowed) + blow pipes at 6–7 bar service air.
5-YearlyAll previous + uncouple all main bottles. Open one pilot bottle and test time delay unit, master valve auto-open, and high-pressure leak alarm. Check manifold relief valve lifts at 180 bar. Pressure test isolated piping sections at 25 bar air.
10-YearlyRenew all flexible hoses. Internal inspection of bottles (check dip tubes). Hydrostatic test at 250 bar — test 10% of bottles; if any fail → test 50%; if more fail → test 100%. After 20 years of age: hydrostatic test every 5 years.

Required Certificates

  • Installation Certificate.
  • Shore maintenance/weighing certificate (every 2 years).
  • Bottle pressure test certificate (every 10 years; every 5 years after 20 years of age).
⚖ Regulatory References
FSS Code Ch. 5 / Sec. 2.2.5CO₂ room location, construction, and ventilation requirements
MSC.1/Circ.1318Revised guidelines for maintenance and inspection of fixed CO₂ fire-extinguishing systems
SOLAS II-2 / Reg 14Operational readiness, maintenance and inspections of fire protection systems
Simon Sir's most-tested CO₂ question: "What is the 10-yearly maintenance of the CO₂ system?" — Renew flexible hoses, internal bottle inspection, hydrostatic test at 250 bar, test 10%/50%/100% batch rule. He will also ask: "Why is the ventilation exhaust at the bottom of the CO₂ room?" — CO₂ is 1.5× heavier than air → sinks → must be extracted from the bottom to prevent accumulation.

TQM — Total Quality Management in Shipping (Pages 152–153)
ISMManagement

Definition

TQM is a comprehensive management philosophy seeking to continually improve products, services, and processes through quality focus and the active involvement of all employees. Quality is not a single department's responsibility — it is a collective effort.

8 Principles of TQM

  • 1. Customer Focus: Align strategy to meet and exceed customer expectations.
  • 2. Continuous Improvement (Kaizen): Ongoing structured efforts to improve processes.
  • 3. Employee Involvement: Every employee participates in quality improvement.
  • 4. Process-Centred Approach: Identify hidden inefficiencies through systematic workflows.
  • 5. Integrated System: All departments work seamlessly toward quality objectives.
  • 6. Fact-Based Decision Making: Decisions based on empirical data, not intuition.
  • 7. Strategic & Systematic Approach: TQM aligned with organisation's long-term goals.
  • 8. Communication: Open channels ensuring everyone understands their role in quality.

TQM Aims in Shipping Operations

  • Zero Cargo Claims; Zero Lost Time Incidents (LTI).
  • Zero Detentions or PSC deficiencies.
  • Minimum operating costs; no MARPOL fines.
  • No major Non-Conformities (NC) under the ISM Code.
  • Safe, high-efficiency cargo turnaround.
⚖ Regulatory References
ISO 9001:2015Quality Management Systems — High Level Structure (HLS) enabling integration with ISM, OHSAS, ISO 14001
ISM Code Sec. 11Documentation requirements — SMS documentation as part of integrated quality management
ISO 14001:2015Environmental Management Systems — integrated with TQM for shipping environmental compliance
Simon Sir typically asks: "What is TQM and what are its principles?" — Give all 8 principles with "Kaizen" for principle 2. He may link it to ISO 9001:2015 — know that the HLS (High Level Structure) of 2015 allows integration of Quality + Safety + Environmental + Energy management systems into one IMS.

GISIS, FAL Convention & Standardised FAL Forms (Pages 154–155)
SurveyCommercial

GISIS — Global Integrated Shipping Information System

IMO's centralised online platform collecting, processing, storing, and sharing regulatory and operational data on global shipping and maritime safety. Used for ship surveys, port state inspections, flag state data.

FAL Convention (1965 / EIF March 1967)

Facilitates international maritime traffic by streamlining administrative formalities, port procedures, and documentary requirements for ships arriving at, staying in, and departing from international ports.

  • Prevent unnecessary delays: Reduce bureaucratic paperwork and time in international maritime traffic.
  • Standardised documentation: Promote global standardisation of clearance documents.
  • Maritime Single Window (MSW): Mandatory from 1 January 2024 — electronic exchange between ships and ports mandatory.

Maritime Single Window & IRS Electronic Certificates (Pages 156–157)
SurveyIACS

Maritime Single Window (MSW)

A unified digital platform for rapid exchange of electronic information between ships, port authorities, customs, immigration, and shipping companies — mandatory from 1 January 2024 under FAL Convention amendments.

  • Single point of entry: All regulatory data submitted through one portal — eliminates repetitive paperwork to multiple government authorities.
  • India — SWIFT: Single Window Interface for Facilitating Trade + NLP-Marine (National Logistics Portal Marine).
  • Singapore — NSW: National Single Window.

IRS Electronic Certificates

  • IRS implemented e-certificates from July 2018 for all vessels classed under IRS.
  • All statutory and class certificates issued as E-Certificates — digitally signed with a Unique Tracking Number (UTN) for online verification.
  • Initially not extended to passenger ships; requires MoU between Flag State and RO.

Advantages of Electronic Certificates

  • Rapid delivery onboard — no physical mailing.
  • Easy document management — no bulky paper folders.
  • No risk of physical document loss, theft, or deterioration.
  • Cyber security must be integrated into ship's SMS (audited during DOC/SMC renewal).
⚖ Regulatory References
FAL.6(18)Amendments to FAL Convention — electronic exchange of information mandatory from April 2019
MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3Guidelines on Maritime Cyber Risk Management
MSC-FAL.1/Circ.8Guidelines for setting up and operating a Maritime Single Window
Simon Sir typically asks: "What is the Maritime Single Window?" — Unified digital port clearance platform; mandatory from 1 January 2024. IRS e-certificate from July 2018 with UTN is a current affairs question. He may ask: "What is a UTN?" — Unique Tracking Number on e-certificates for instant online authenticity verification.

Five Categories of Maritime Liens & True Maritime Lien (Pages 158–159)
CommercialAdmiralty

Five Categories of Maritime Liens

  • 1. Maritime Lien (True): Attaches to crew wages, salvage, or physical damage caused by ship's action.
  • 2. Statutory Lien: Government/statutory bodies for port charges, customs dues. Valid for 6 months (60 days after sale of vessel, whichever is earlier).
  • 3. Possessory Lien: Arises while holding physical possession — ship repair yard, ship chandler claims. Lost if possession is surrendered.
  • 4. Contractual Lien: Established via express contract terms — shipowner's lien on cargo for unpaid freight.
  • 5. Judicial Lien: Generated by court order — attachment lien, execution lien.

Maritime lien can attach only to: The Ship, The Cargo, or The Freight.

True Maritime Lien — Key Characteristics

  • Privileged claim: Priority over ship mortgages, possessory liens, and statutory rights in rem.
  • Secret and indelible: Travels with the property even if the vessel is sold — invisible, not registered in any public registry.
  • Accrues automatically at the moment of the event giving rise to the cause of action.
  • Enforcement: By Action in Rem — the ship (res) is physically arrested by the Admiralty Court and may be sold to satisfy creditors.

Legal Glossary

  • Res: Latin for "thing" — the maritime property (ship, cargo, freight).
  • Action in Rem: Legal action directed against the property itself.
  • Action in Personam: Legal action against a person, company, or entity.
  • Pari Passu: Claims of the same class rank equally — proceeds shared proportionately.
⚖ Regulatory References
Admiralty Act 2017 (India)Admiralty (Jurisdiction and Settlement of Maritime Claims) Act — replaces archaic British admiralty statutes
Admiralty Act 2017 / Sec. 9Maritime liens — priority, nature, enforcement, and expiry provisions
UNCLOS Art. 113–114Damage by submarine cables/pipelines — liability provisions relevant to maritime claims
Simon Sir (or Rajappan/Nair) typically asks: "What are the types of maritime liens?" — Give all 5 types. Key distinction: True maritime lien = secret + indelible + travels with ship even after sale. Statutory lien expires in 6 months. Possessory lien is lost if possession is surrendered. "Pari Passu" = equal ranking within the same class — must know the Latin term.

Priority of Maritime Liens & Admiralty Act 2017 (Pages 160–162)
CommercialAdmiralty

Common True Maritime Lien Claims (in rank order)

  • Salvage claims.
  • Crew wages and Master's disbursements.
  • Damage done by a ship (collision claims).
  • Bottomry and Respondentia bonds.

Bottomry Bond: Ship pledged as collateral by the Master to secure emergency funds.
Respondentia Bond: Cargo pledged as collateral.

Priority Rule — "Last in Time, First in Right"

Last in Time, First in Right (Most recent lien preserving the vessel takes precedence)

  • Salvage claims: ranked in inverse chronological order — latest salvage operation has highest priority.
  • Liens of the same class: rank Pari Passu (equal, proportional payment).

Admiralty Act 2017 (India) — Key Provisions

  • Maritime lien continues even if vessel changes ownership, registration, or flag.
  • General maritime lien expiry: 1 year unless the vessel is seized and sold by the High Court.
  • Wage-related claims: 2 years from date claim becomes due (wages, repatriation, contractual sums).
  • Statutory lien: 6 months; 60 days after sale, whichever expires first.

Admiralty Assessor Rules 2018 — Qualifications

  • MEO Class 1 Engineer.
  • Sea experience including 5+ years in survey and inspection of merchant ships; at least 1 year as CE or 2E.
  • Appeal: Plaintiff may approach the Supreme Court of India.
⚖ Regulatory References
Admiralty Act 2017 / Sec. 9–10Maritime liens — rank, priority, and duration of various categories of maritime claims
Admiralty Act 2017 / Sec. 22Inter se priority — ranking of maritime liens of the same and different classes
Admiralty Assessors Rules 2018Qualifications and appointment of Admiralty Assessors in Indian High Courts
Key exam points: "Last in Time, First in Right" principle for maritime liens of different classes. "Pari Passu" for same class. Duration: 1 year (general), 2 years (wages), 6 months (statutory). Admiralty Assessor: MEO Class 1 + 5 years survey experience — this is directly relevant to your career path.

UNCLOS Maritime Zones, EEZ & International Seabed Authority (Pages 163–166)
UNCLOSCommercial

UNCLOS III (1982) — Maritime Zones

Baseline ├─ Territorial Sea (TS): up to 12 NM ├─ Contiguous Zone: up to 24 NM ├─ Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ): up to 200 NM (excl. TS) └─ Continental Shelf: to outer continental margin Max: 350 NM from baseline OR 100 NM seaward of the 2,500m isobath (whichever is further) Area Beyond National Jurisdiction (ABNJ): beyond all the above

Key UNCLOS Articles

  • Art. 33: Contiguous Zone — coastal state may enforce customs, fiscal, immigration, sanitary laws.
  • Art. 56: EEZ rights — sovereign rights to exploit natural resources; jurisdiction over artificial islands, marine research, environment protection.
  • Art. 77: Continental shelf — sovereign rights for exploring and exploiting natural resources.
  • Art. 192: General obligation to protect and preserve the marine environment.
  • Art. 195: No transfer of hazards — states must not transfer pollution from one area to another.
  • Art. 218/220: Port State and Coastal State enforcement of environmental regulations.

International Seabed Authority (ISA)

  • Established under UNCLOS — headquartered in Kingston, Jamaica.
  • Regulates all mineral-related activities in the international seabed area (The Area — ABNJ).
  • All UNCLOS state parties are ISA members.
⚖ Regulatory References
UNCLOS 1982 / Art. 55–75EEZ — rights, jurisdiction, duties of coastal states; due regard for other states
UNCLOS 1982 / Art. 192–196Environmental protection obligations — prevent, reduce, control marine pollution
Maritime Zones Act 1976 (India)Indian legislation defining maritime boundaries aligned with UNCLOS provisions
Simon Sir / Nair typically asks: "What are the maritime zones under UNCLOS?" — Give all zones with distances from baseline. Key articles: 94 (Flag State duties), 192 (protect marine environment), 218/220 (PSC/coastal state enforcement). ISA headquarters: Kingston, Jamaica.

CSC Convention, Safety Approval Plate & Inspection Schemes (Pages 167–168)
SOLASContainer

CSC Convention

  • Adopted: December 1972; EIF: September 1977.
  • Convened jointly by UN and IMO.
  • BIC (Bureau International des Containers): Non-profit organisation assigning owner prefixes and managing the BIC Code Register. Follows ISO 6346 for container coding, identification, and marking.

CSC Safety Approval Plate

Attached to the outside left door of the container. Contains:

  • "CSC SAFETY APPROVAL" stamping (bold characters ≥ 8mm height).
  • Classification company approval code, manufacturing date, manufacturer's container ID number.
  • Maximum Gross Weight: Container + cargo weight safely transportable.
  • Stacking Load Value: Weight stackable on top at 1.8G force.
  • Racking Test Load Value: Transverse force before frame twists.
  • First maintenance examination date.

Container Weight Formulas

Gross Weight = Tare Weight + Cargo Weight

Payload = Maximum cargo weight the container can hold

Container Inspection Schemes

  • PES (Periodic Examination Scheme): First exam ≤ 5 years after manufacture; thereafter at intervals ≤ 30 months. Next inspection date marked on CSC plate.
  • ACEP (Approved Continuous Examination Programme): Container examined every time repair is carried out; must align with PES intervals minimum; ACEP number displayed on plate.

CSC plate validity note: A CSC plate is valid only if the container is in good order. Any authorised agent can take a container out of service immediately if damaged to a dangerous level — regardless of inspection scheme.

⚖ Regulatory References
CSC Convention 1972International Convention for Safe Containers — safety approval, inspection, and marking requirements
ISO 6346Freight containers — coding, identification, and marking including owner prefix and equipment identifier
SOLAS VI / Reg 2Safe loading and securing — container CSC compliance required before loading on ships
Simon Sir / Rajappan typically asks: "What information is on the CSC Safety Approval Plate?" — List all items; emphasise it is on the outside left door. He may ask the difference between PES and ACEP — PES = periodic time-based; ACEP = repair-triggered + continuous. CSC plate validity depends on condition, not just inspection date.

Container Marking (ISO 6346), Identification & Large Ship Problems (Pages 169–170)
SOLASContainer

Container Marking Structure (ISO 6346)

  • Owner's Prefix: 3 capital letters (owner/operator).
  • Equipment Category Identifier: 1 capital letter:
    • U = All freight containers.
    • J = Detachable freight container-related equipment.
    • Z = Trailers and chassis carrying storage containers.
  • Serial Number: 6-digit unique number within owner's fleet.
  • Check Digit: Single digit validating accuracy of prefix and serial number.

Problems Associated with Large Container Ships

  • Stacking of light containers & Windage: Large above-deck container stacks catch wind — reduces manoeuvrability (e.g., Ever Given grounding, Suez Canal 2021).
  • Torsional forces & Parametric Rolling: G-forces during pitch and roll cause container stack failures and loss overboard.
  • Infrastructure demands: Massive capital investment required (e.g., Panama Canal expansion 2016 = $5 billion).

Prototype Tests on Containers (carried out regardless of single or series build)

  • Dimension check; Lifting test (top corner, bottom corner, forklift).
  • Stacking test; Concentrated load on roof and floor.
  • Transverse racking test; Longitudinal restraint test.
  • Endwall and sidewall test; Door operation test.
  • Cargo securing device test; Weather tightness test.
⚖ Regulatory References
ISO 6346Container coding, identification, and marking — owner prefix, equipment identifier, serial, check digit
CSC Convention 1972Prototype testing requirements for new container designs and series production
SOLAS VI / Reg 5-1Container weight verification — VGM mandatory before loading since 2016
Simon Sir typically asks: "What are the prototype tests for containers?" — List all 9+ tests; emphasise they apply irrespective of whether a single container or a series is built. He may ask about windage on large container ships — direct link to the Ever Given. Know container ID structure: 3+1+6+1 (XXXU-123456-7).

Container Logistics, VGM, Reefer PTI & New Regulations (Pages 171–174)
SOLASContainer

Key Logistics Definitions

  • Liner Trade: Fixed routes, strict schedules, published timetables — predictable for exporters/importers.
  • SOC: Shipper Owned Container; COC: Carrier Owned Container (negotiated under NVOCC).
  • ICD (Inland Container Depot): Inland site where shipping and customs clearance for containerised cargo can be processed.
  • CFS (Container Freight Station): Physical extension of the port for temporary storage.

VGM — Verified Gross Mass (SOLAS Ch. VI, from 2016)

Shippers must declare the actual weight before loading. Without VGM, container is denied loading.

  • Method 1: Direct weighing on a certified weighbridge — VGM = total weight of packed, sealed container.
  • Method 2: Calculated — VGM = Σ(individual package weights + dunnage/securing materials) + container tare weight.
  • Weighing equipment must meet National Certification and Calibration requirements (India: Legal Metrology Dept).

Reefer Container PTI (Pre-Trip Inspection)

  • Functional check on empty refrigerated container before release to shipper.
  • Valid PTI certificate: 30 to 180 days (varies by carrier).
  • Checklist: cooling unit, temperature recording device, structural integrity, cleaning, connection to main control module.

New Container Ship Regulations (2026)

  • Electronic Inclinometers: Mandatory from January 2026 for container ships and bulk carriers > 7,000 GT.
  • Mandatory Reporting of Lost Containers: Strictly mandatory from January 2026.

Fire Fighting for Containers (Post-2016 ships)

  • At least one water mist lance for container fires in open spaces.
  • Two water monitors if ship beam ≤ 30 m; four monitors if beam > 30 m.
  • Thermal imaging camera mandatory.
⚖ Regulatory References
SOLAS VI / Reg 2VGM — Verified Gross Mass; shippers must verify and provide weight before loading; mandatory from 2016
SOLAS II-2 / Reg 10.10Portable monitors and water mist lances for fighting fires in cargo areas
MSC.380(94)Amendments to SOLAS VI Regulation 2 — VGM provision, responsibilities of shippers and masters
Simon Sir / Rajappan typically ask: "What is VGM and what are the two methods?" — Method 1 = certified weighbridge; Method 2 = calculated sum + tare weight. Both methods require certified/calibrated equipment. 2026 regulations (inclinometers + lost container reporting) are current affairs hot topics for container vessel orals at Kochi MMD.
