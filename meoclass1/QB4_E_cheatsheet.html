⚡ QB4_E Cheat Sheet

ISM, MLC, SOLAS, PSC & Class — 15 questions · Kochi MMD, 29 June 2026 · Examiners: Nair, Simon, Srivastava

📋 MLC 2006 & Seafarer Welfare

Q1 — MLC Titles & RPSL

MLC structure: Articles → Regulations → Code (Part A mandatory Standards, Part B recommended Guidelines). 5 Titles: (1) Minimum requirements, (2) Employment conditions, (3) Accommodation/food, (4) Health/welfare, (5) Compliance/enforcement. RPSL = India's national recruitment licensing regime (predates MLC's 2013 entry into force). India ratified MLC 9 Oct 2015. Trap: forgetting Title 5, or saying MLC = "Regulations/Standards/Guidelines" only (missing Articles/Code).

Q2 — MLC Financial Security

Two heads: Std A2.5.2 (repatriation security — incl. up to 4 months' unpaid wages) and Std A4.2 (shipowners' liability — death/long-term disability compensation). Ship must carry evidence, PSC can inspect.

Q3 — Young Seafarer

Under MLC, a young person is under 18. No night work, no hazardous work (heat/noise/vibration/toxic substances), and medical fitness re-certified more frequently. CE's practical duty: verify age documentation, restrict watch/task assignment, monitor rest hours strictly.

⚓ SOLAS

Q4 — Latest SOLAS Amendments

Verify the exact current-cycle amendments against the latest MSC session before your exam — this answer set is the most time-sensitive item in the whole bank and goes stale fastest. Know the general MSC/MEPC amendment adoption-to-entry-into-force cycle (tacit acceptance, typically ~2 years) even if specific circular numbers slip your memory.

📋 ISM Code

Q5 — ISM 12 Elements

Know all 12 by number and name — Nair expects the sequence, not just topics. Be ready to expand Elements 1 (General), 5 (Master's responsibility), 7 (Shipboard operations), 8 (Emergency preparedness), 9 (Non-conformities), 10 (Maintenance), 12 (Company verification/review) in detail.

Q6 — Master's Overriding Authority

Element 5 — Master has absolute overriding authority and responsibility for safety and pollution prevention decisions, and may deviate from company procedures when necessary for safety/environment. Company must not constrain this authority.

Q7 — Continuous Improvement

Driven by: internal/external audits (Element 12), non-conformity reports and root-cause analysis (Element 9), Master's review, management review, near-miss reporting culture, and updating the SMS based on findings — the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle underlies ISM.

🔍 Port State Control & Survey

Q8 — PSC Appeal & Detention

On detention: rectify deficiencies, request re-inspection, right to appeal to the detaining authority/Flag State. Know the relevant MOU (Tokyo/Paris etc.) inspection regime and deficiency-code structure. Detention triggers Flag State and Class notification.

Q9 — Age Norms for Ships

Ship age affects enhanced survey scheme requirements (ESP), CAP ratings, insurance/P&I scrutiny, and some port/charterer age restrictions. Distinguish from young-seafarer age norms (Q3) — don't conflate the two "age" topics if the examiner chains them.

🧭 STCW

Q10 — STCW/MLC Assessor

Under STCW A-I/6, an assessor must hold appropriate qualifications/experience for what they're assessing and be trained in assessment methods. As CE, you act as a certified assessor signing off TRB entries — based on direct observation, not just elapsed time.

🔧 Classification & IACS

Q11 — Class Notations

Structure your answer: main class symbol + hull/machinery notation, then additional notations (ESP, ice class, automation/UMS notation, environmental notations). Always be ready to explain your own vessel's actual notation string component by component.

Q12 — IACS Members

12 current members (2026): ABS (USA), BV (France), CCS (China), CRS (Croatia), DNV (Norway), IRClass (India), KR (South Korea), LR (UK), ClassNK (Japan), PRS (Poland), RINA (Italy), TL (Türkiye). RMRS (Russia) was expelled March 2022 — don't list them. Türk Loydu joined Nov 2023, restoring count to 12. Trap: the commonly-circulated "11 member" list is outdated — always double check you have 12, including KR.

🔍 Registration & Manning

Q13 — India Ship Registration

Registered under the Merchant Shipping Act with the Registrar/DG Shipping-designated port office. Certificates issued: Certificate of Registry, plus statutory certificates via Class/RO (IOPP, SMC, DOC, Load Line, etc.). Know who issues what — DG Shipping vs. delegated Class RO.

Q14 — Crew Complement

Decided via the Safe Manning Document, issued by the Flag State based on STCW watchkeeping requirements, vessel automation/UMS notation, and company SMS operational needs. Not a fixed number — varies by ship type, trade, and automation level.

⚡ Cyber Security

Q15 — Cyber Security Management

Anchor to ISM Code (cyber risk integrated into the SMS since 2021, per MSC.428(98)) and BIMCO Cyber Security Guidelines. Key CE-relevant points: segregation of IT/OT networks, USB/removable media control on ECDIS and engine automation systems, patch management, incident reporting chain to the DPA.

Common Thread

Nair's Pattern

Nair chains topics fluidly within one session (MLC → RPSL → ISM Elements → PSC) — stay ready for topic jumps. He rewards regulation numbers stated verbatim and structured, numbered answers over prose. The two most time-sensitive items in this bank are Q4 (SOLAS amendments) and Q15 (cyber security) — re-verify both against current IMO circulars close to your exam date.
