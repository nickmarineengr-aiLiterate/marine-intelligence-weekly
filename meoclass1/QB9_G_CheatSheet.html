Concept Map — How a Convention Becomes Binding Law

Convention (Treaty) → Protocol is a structural add-on anywhere in this chain that needs its own separate ratification. Once in force, most technical amendments skip the slow left-hand path and pass by Tacit Acceptance instead (deemed accepted unless enough Parties object).

Confusable Pairs — Examiner Favourites

⚠ Confusable Pair: In Rem vs In Personam
In Rem = suit against the ship itself, enables arrest, purely territorial (ship just needs to be in Indian waters). In Personam = suit against the owner/company, attaches to their general assets. In rem recovery is capped at the ship's value/freight — anything more needs a separate in personam claim.
⚠ Confusable Pair: Admiralty Act S.9 vs S.10
S.9 defines a maritime lien and its own inter se ranking (1-year extinction unless arrested first). S.10 sets the broader claims priority order used in a judicial-sale distribution. Don't cite S.9 when asked for the general claims-priority ranking.
⚠ Confusable Pair: Convention vs Protocol
A Convention is the parent multilateral treaty (SOLAS, MARPOL). A Protocol has identical binding force but is a separate instrument needing its own ratification — ratifying the parent does not automatically bind a state to the Protocol. MARPOL 73/78 is the classic exception: the 1978 Protocol absorbed the unratified 1973 Convention.
⚠ Confusable Pair: General Average vs Particular Average
GA = deliberate, voluntary sacrifice for common safety, shared by all interests (S.66, Marine Insurance Act). PA = accidental, partial loss to one asset, borne solely by that owner/insurer (S.64). A thrown-rod engine failure itself is PA; the salvage tow that follows to save the whole ship is GA.
⚠ Confusable Pair: Official Log Book vs Engine Log Book
The OLB is the one statutory legal document per vessel, Master's custody, admissible in court. The Engine Log Book is an operational technical journal with no independent statutory legal status — it only matters legally once reflected in the OLB.

Topic Clusters — Tap to Recall

Admiralty Jurisdiction & Ship Arrest
Q540. Acts besides MS Act — Admiralty Act contents
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
Admiralty Act 2017, Recycling of Ships Act 2019, Major Port Authorities Act 2021, Marine Insurance Act 1963. Admiralty Act 2017: S.3 jurisdiction to coastal High Courts, S.4 maritime claims, S.5 in rem arrest (incl. sister ships), S.9 maritime lien ranking, S.10 broader claims priority.
Q541. In Rem vs In Personam
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
In rem = suit against the vessel itself, enables arrest regardless of owner's location. In personam = suit against a specific person/company, attaches to their general assets, not just the ship.
IMO Instrument Process
Q548. How an IMO Convention is Adopted
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
Proposal → Sub-Committee → Committee (MSC/MEPC/LEG/FAL/TC) → Diplomatic Conference → Signature → Ratification/Acceptance/Approval/Accession (all equal legal effect) → Entry into Force. Post entry-into-force amendments mostly pass via Tacit Acceptance, not fresh ratification.
Q554. Convention vs Protocol vs Treaty
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
Treaty = umbrella legal term. Convention = broad multilateral standard (SOLAS, MARPOL). Protocol = major structural add-on/update, same binding force, own ratification (MARPOL 73/78, SOLAS Protocol 1988, 1997 Protocol → Annex VI).
Statutory Documentation & Digitalisation
Q551. Official Log Book
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
Statutory legal document, Master's custody, records crew/disciplinary/casualty/medical events. One per vessel — distinct from the operational Engine/Deck Log Book. Corrections: single-line strike-through + initials, never erased.
Q552. Maritime Single Window & FAL
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
Single electronic portal for port clearance data (customs/immigration/health/port authority). Mandatory worldwide from 1 January 2024 under the FAL Convention's amended Annex. Master remains responsible for data accuracy even via a shore agent.
Marine Insurance & Liability
Q560. General Average vs Particular Average
Tap to reveal the 15-second answer →
GA = deliberate, reasonable sacrifice for common safety, shared proportionally by ship/cargo/freight (York-Antwerp Rules, S.66 Marine Insurance Act). PA = accidental, partial loss to one interest, borne solely by that owner/insurer (S.64).
