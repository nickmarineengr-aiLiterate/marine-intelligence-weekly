Define Special Area and PSSA under MARPOL/IMO, with examples.
MARPOL|PSSA
15-Second AnswerA Special Area is a MARPOL-designated zone with mandatory strict discharge limits (Annexes I, II, IV, V); a PSSA is a separate IMO Resolution A.982(24) designation for ecologically/socially significant areas needing protective traffic measures.
60-Second AnswerSpecial Areas sit inside MARPOL Annexes I, II, IV and V and impose stricter discharge criteria due to oceanographic and traffic characteristics. PSSAs are an IMO Assembly designation under Resolution A.982(24), independent of any single Annex, requiring Associated Protective Measures like routing schemes or mandatory pilotage. Some zones, like the Baltic and North Sea, are designated as both simultaneously.

Regulatory Context

A Special Area (SA) is defined within MARPOL Annexes I, II, IV and V as a sea area requiring mandatory stricter discharge methods due to oceanographic/ecological condition and traffic density. A Particularly Sensitive Sea Area (PSSA) is a separate IMO designation under Resolution A.982(24) for areas needing protection for ecological, socio-economic or scientific significance vulnerable to shipping.

Key Distinctions

  • SA — standardised strict discharge limits set by the relevant Annex (e.g. Mediterranean, Baltic).
  • PSSA — custom Associated Protective Measures (APMs) such as traffic separation schemes, mandatory pilotage or ship reporting (e.g. Great Barrier Reef, first PSSA in 1990; the Torres Strait was added as a separate extension in 2005 via MEPC.133(53), not designated simultaneously with the Reef).
  • An area can be both (e.g. Baltic Sea, North Sea).

PSSA Designation Criteria

At least one of: ecological (uniqueness, critical habitat), socio-economic (fisheries, tourism dependency), or scientific/educational significance, combined with demonstrated vulnerability to international shipping.

Key Numbers

PSSA governing instrument: Resolution A.982(24). First PSSA: Great Barrier Reef, 1990 — Torres Strait added as a separate extension in 2005 (MEPC.133(53)). Do not state these as one combined 1990 event.
Regulatory References
MARPOL Art. 2 &amp; Annexes I, II, IV, VFramework establishing Special Areas
IMO Resolution A.982(24)Guidelines for identification/designation of PSSAs
IMO Resolution A.1087(28)Guidelines for designation of Special Areas
MEPC.133(53)Extension of GBR PSSA to include Torres Strait (2005)
SOLAS Ch. V, Reg. 10 &amp; 11Ships' routing and reporting systems used as APMs
CE Oral Tip: Simon probes the legal distinction directly — do not conflate the two. State plainly: Special Areas are MARPOL discharge-limit zones; PSSAs are IMO Assembly-designated protective-measure zones. Cite the Baltic for SA and Great Barrier Reef for PSSA on reflex.
CE Relevance

Entering a Special Area, verify bilge/bunker operations meet the stricter discharge criteria; entering a PSSA, confirm bridge-engine room alignment on Associated Protective Measures such as mandatory routing or pilotage.

Trap Questions

"Can an area be both a Special Area and a PSSA?" — Yes; the Baltic and North Sea are both, combining strict discharge limits with specialised routing/traffic measures.

Common CE Failures
  • Stating PSSAs are defined under MARPOL Annex I (incorrect — separate IMO Assembly Resolution)
  • Being unable to name a concrete example for each
Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • A.982(24): PSSA governing resolution
  • 15 ppm: maximum bilge discharge under Annex I where permitted en route
Casualty Link

Shen Neng 1 (2010): grounding on the Great Barrier Reef reinforced enforcement of PSSA Associated Protective Measures including mandatory tracking and pilotage.

What statutory certificates are required under MARPOL Annex VI?
MARPOL VI|CII
15-Second AnswerMARPOL Annex VI requires the IAPP and IEE Certificates plus annual DCS and CII Statements of Compliance for ships ≥400 GT (DCS/CII from ≥5,000 GT), supported by EIAPP certificates, BDNs and an ODS Record Book where applicable.
60-Second AnswerFor ships 400 GT and above, the IAPP Certificate (SOx/NOx/PM/ODS, 5-year validity) and IEE Certificate (EEDI/EEXI, life-of-ship validity, tied to SEEMP) are mandatory. Ships also carry annual Statements of Compliance for IMO DCS fuel reporting (SEEMP Part II) and, above 5,000 GT, for CII operational rating (SEEMP Part III). Supporting records include per-engine EIAPP certificates, Bunker Delivery Notes kept 3 years, and fuel samples kept 1 year.

Regulatory Context

For ships of 400 GT and above on international voyages, MARPOL Annex VI requires a defined set of statutory certificates verifying air-pollution and energy-efficiency compliance.

Primary Certificates

  • IAPP Certificate — SOx/NOx/PM/ODS compliance, with a Record of Construction and Equipment supplement; valid max 5 years.
  • IEE Certificate — EEDI/EEXI attainment per Reg. 5.4, requiring an onboard SEEMP; valid for ship's life absent major conversion.
  • Statement of Compliance — IMO DCS — annual fuel-consumption data verification (SEEMP Part II).
  • Statement of Compliance — CII rating — for ships ≥5,000 GT, annual carbon-intensity rating A-E (SEEMP Part III).

Supporting Records

  • EIAPP Certificate per engine >130 kW, with Engine Technical File and Parameter Record Book.
  • Bunker Delivery Notes (retained ≥3 years) and MARPOL fuel samples (retained ≥1 year or until substantially consumed).
  • ODS Record Book where ODS-containing equipment is fitted.

Key Numbers

400 GT IAPP/IEE threshold; 5,000 GT DCS/CII threshold; BDN retention 3 years; fuel sample retention 1 year.
Regulatory References
MARPOL Annex VI, Regs. 5-9Surveys, certification and validity
MARPOL Annex VI, Regs. 20-28EEDI, EEXI, SEEMP, DCS and CII
NOx Technical Code 2008Testing/certification of marine diesel engines
DGS India MS Notice 04/2023National guidance on SEEMP/CII implementation
CE Oral Tip: Nair focuses on the IAPP Supplement content. Be ready to explain the SOx checkbox (low-sulphur fuel vs EGCS/scrubber) and NOx tier selection, and never confuse the engine-specific EIAPP with the ship-wide IAPP.
CE Relevance

Maintain Engine Technical Files and Parameter Record Books so no unauthorised modification invalidates the EIAPP; present IAPP, BDNs and ORB Part II/engine logbook as a consistent set during PSC.

Trap Questions

"Does swapping a fuel injector for a non-identical part affect certification?" — Yes, it can invalidate the EIAPP if it modifies emission-critical components specified in the Engine Technical File.

Common CE Failures
  • Missing or unverified SEEMP Part III during PSC on modern vessels
  • Confusing EIAPP (per-engine) with IAPP (ship-wide)
Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • 400 GT: IAPP/IEE threshold
  • 5,000 GT: DCS/CII threshold
  • 3 years: BDN retention
  • 1 year: fuel sample retention
Casualty Link

Several vessel detentions (notably USCG) have resulted from falsified ODS logs or engines run with disabled emission controls outside certified parameters.

Examiner Chain (Kochi MMD)

Nair: IAPP scope → supplement contents → IEE/SEEMP link → DCS/CII statements.

On My Vessel (Maersk Container)

Digital fuel-flow feeds support IMO DCS/CII reporting fleet-wide; entries are cross-checked against physical soundings and BDNs held in the ship's central file.

What is the discharge criteria for oil tankers under MARPOL Annex I?
MARPOL I|ODME
15-Second AnswerMARPOL Annex I Reg. 34 permits oil-tanker cargo-space discharge only outside Special Areas, en route, beyond 50 NM, at ≤30 L/NM and ≤1/30,000 of cargo total, with an operating ODME — distinct from Reg. 15's 15 ppm machinery-space limit.
60-Second AnswerRegulation 34 sets cumulative conditions for tanker cargo-space discharge: outside a Special Area, en route, more than 50 NM from land, instantaneous rate not exceeding 30 L/NM, total quantity not exceeding 1/30,000 of the cargo, with an operational ODME enforcing automatic shutoff. This is separate from Regulation 15's machinery-space rule of under 15 ppm oil content for all ships.

Regulatory Context

MARPOL Annex I, Regulation 34 governs discharge of oily mixtures from cargo tank spaces of oil tankers, distinct from Regulation 15 which covers machinery-space bilge discharge on all ships.

Regulation 34 — Cargo Space Discharge

All of the following must apply simultaneously:

  • Outside a designated Special Area
  • En route
  • More than 50 nautical miles from nearest land
  • Instantaneous rate ≤ 30 litres per nautical mile
  • Total quantity ≤ 1/30,000 of the total cargo quantity from which the residue formed
  • Operational Oil Discharge Monitoring & Control system (ODME) in use, with automatic shutoff on breach

Regulation 15 — Machinery Space (all ships ≥400 GT)

Oil content must be below 15 ppm, en route, with an approved 15 ppm separator and alarm (automatic stopping device required inside Special Areas).

Formula

Instantaneous Rate (L/NM) = [Oil content (ppm) × Discharge flow (m³/hr)] ÷ [1000 × Ship speed (knots)]

Key Numbers

50 NM from land; 30 L/NM instantaneous rate; 1/30,000 total quantity fraction; machinery bilge 15 ppm.
Regulatory References
MARPOL Annex I, Reg. 34Discharge criteria — cargo spaces of oil tankers
MARPOL Annex I, Reg. 15Discharge criteria — machinery spaces, all ships
Res. MEPC.108(49) as amendedGuidelines/specifications for ODME
Merchant Shipping (Prevention of Pollution by Oil) Rules — IndiaNational implementing rules
CE Oral Tip: Simon traps candidates on terminology — the cargo-space rate is 30 litres per nautical mile, not '30 ppm'. Be ready to write the ODME rate formula on the board if asked.
CE Relevance

Even on a container vessel, mastering this distinction demonstrates command of MARPOL depth; where relevant, the CE co-owns ODME calibration integrity with the Chief Officer on a tanker.

Trap Questions

"Can clean/segregated ballast be discharged inside a Special Area or within 50 NM?" — Yes; clean ballast (no visible trace, under 15 ppm) and SBT do not constitute an 'oily mixture' under the restrictive Reg. 34 definition.

Common CE Failures
  • Forgetting the 50 NM distance condition for cargo-space discharge
  • Conflating the 15 ppm machinery-space limit with the 30 L/NM cargo-space rate
Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • 50 NM: distance from land
  • 30 L/NM: instantaneous rate
  • 1/30,000: total quantity fraction
  • 15 ppm: machinery-space limit
Casualty Link

Multiple DOJ/USCG enforcement actions have targeted vessels that bypassed the ODME or manipulated speed inputs to inflate the legally permitted discharge allowance ('magic pipe' cases).

Examiner Chain (Kochi MMD)

Simon: Reg. 34 conditions → instantaneous rate formula → ODME function → contrast with Reg. 15 machinery limit.

On My Vessel (Maersk Container)

On Maersk container vessels the Oil Record Book Part I for machinery spaces is strictly maintained and the 15 ppm oily-water separator is never bypassed; cargo-space rules do not directly apply.

Describe MARPOL Annex V record books and the procedure followed onboard.
MARPOL V|Garbage
15-Second AnswerMARPOL Annex V requires ships ≥100 GT (or ≥15 persons certified, or any fixed/floating platform) to keep a two-part Garbage Record Book — Part I for all operational waste, Part II for cargo residues on bulk carriers only — supported by a Garbage Management Plan with strict segregation and logging procedures.
60-Second AnswerThe Garbage Record Book has Part I, applicable to every ship above the threshold, logging discharge or incineration of plastics, food waste, domestic waste, ash, non-HME cargo residues and fishing gear; and Part II for solid-bulk-cargo residue-generating vessels — bulk carriers, not tankers, whose cargo tank washings fall under Annex I/II instead. Onboard, waste is segregated at source per the Garbage Management Plan, processed via incinerator/compactor or shore reception, and every event is logged with position, quantity and dual sign-off.
Verify before quoting: Threshold was lowered from 400 GT to 100 GT and above (or ≥15 persons, or any fixed/floating platform) under MEPC.360(79), in force 1 May 2024. This is a live "recent amendment" trap — 400 GT is the outdated figure.

Regulatory Context

MARPOL Annex V requires ships of 100 GT and above (raised from the earlier 400 GT threshold by MEPC.360(79), effective 1 May 2024), or certified to carry 15 or more persons, or any fixed/floating platform, to maintain a two-part Garbage Record Book (GRB) supported by a Garbage Management Plan (GMP).

GRB Structure

  • Part I (all ships): logs discharges/incineration of plastics, food waste, domestic waste, cooking oil, incinerator ash, non-HME cargo residues, operational waste, animal carcasses and fishing gear.
  • Part II (bulk carriers only): cargo residues and associated tank washings from solid bulk cargoes. Tankers are not covered here — tanker cargo tank washings fall under MARPOL Annex I/II, not Annex V Part II.

Onboard Procedure

  • Segregation at source into colour-coded bins per the GMP.
  • Processing via approved equipment — incinerator, compactor, or shore reception.
  • Every discharge, incineration or accidental loss logged with date, position (GPS), category and quantity, signed by the officer in charge and countersigned by the Master.

Key Numbers

Threshold: 100 GT (MEPC.360(79), eff. 1 May 2024) or ≥15 persons certified capacity, or any platform. Part II applies to bulk carriers only (solid bulk cargo), not tankers. GRB retention: 2 years after last entry.
Regulatory References
MARPOL Annex V, Reg. 6 &amp; 7Garbage Record Book Parts I and II
MARPOL Annex V, Reg. 10, as amended by MEPC.360(79)GRB threshold lowered to 100 GT, eff. 1 May 2024; Garbage Management Plan requirement
MARPOL Annex V, Reg. 3-5Discharge/disposal restrictions by garbage category
CE Oral Tip: Nair checks whether candidates know the split between Part I and Part II, and whether every accidental loss (e.g. lost fishing gear) is a mandatory GRB entry, not just intentional discharges.
CE Relevance

The Chief Engineer typically owns Part I entries for engine-room-generated waste (oily rags, incinerator ash) and ensures the incinerator's operating log matches GRB entries for PSC consistency.

Trap Questions

"Is accidental loss of fishing gear a reportable event?" — Yes; accidental loss of fishing gear posing a risk to the environment must be logged in the GRB with position and estimated quantity.

Common CE Failures
  • Omitting GPS position on a discharge/loss entry
  • Confusing which vessel types require Part II (it is bulk carriers, not tankers)
  • Quoting the outdated 400 GT threshold instead of the current 100 GT (MEPC.360(79))
Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • 100 GT / 15 persons / any platform: GRB threshold (MEPC.360(79), eff. 1 May 2024 — lowered from 400 GT)
  • 2 years: retention after last entry
  • Bulk carriers only: Part II scope (not tankers)
Casualty Link

Numerous PSC detentions have followed GRB entries that did not reconcile with actual incinerator operating hours or bin waste logs, exposing falsification.

Examiner Chain (Kochi MMD)

Nair: GRB structure → Part I vs Part II scope → onboard segregation procedure → signing/retention rule.

On My Vessel (Maersk Container)

On Maersk container vessels, colour-coded segregation bins are inspected weekly, and the incinerator log is cross-checked against GRB Part I entries during internal ISM audits.

Discuss the Hong Kong Convention and India's perspective on ship recycling ratification and implementation.
Hong Kong Convention|Ship Recycling
15-Second AnswerThe Hong Kong Convention applies to ships of 500 GT and above on international voyages, and entered into force globally on 26 June 2025; India implemented it via the Recycling of Ships Act, 2019, upgrading Alang yards and requiring an Inventory of Hazardous Materials and Recycling Plan for every vessel scrapped.
60-Second AnswerThe Hong Kong Convention, adopted in 2009, applies to ships of 500 GT and above on international voyages, and entered into force on 26 June 2025 once tonnage and recycling-capacity thresholds were met. India ratified early and enacted the Recycling of Ships Act, 2019, upgrading Alang-Sosiya yards to compliant status ahead of global entry into force, making India the leading HKC-compliant recycling nation. Every vessel now carries a maintained IHM and, at end of life, a yard-approved Recycling Plan before a Ready for Recycling Certificate is issued.

Regulatory Context

The Hong Kong International Convention for the Safe and Environmentally Sound Recycling of Ships, 2009 applies to ships of 500 GT and above on international voyages, and entered into force globally on 26 June 2025, having met the tonnage/capacity ratification thresholds.

India's Implementation

  • India ratified the Convention and enacted the Recycling of Ships Act, 2019, giving it domestic legal force ahead of global entry into force.
  • Alang-Sosiya (Gujarat) yards were upgraded to HKC-compliant status, driving India's position as the world's largest ship-recycling hub by volume.
  • Requirements include an Inventory of Hazardous Materials (IHM) onboard, a ship-specific Recycling Plan approved before dismantling, and a Ready for Recycling Certificate.

Operational Impact

Vessels destined for recycling must maintain and update the IHM through their life, culminating in a final IHM survey before delivery to a compliant yard.

Key Numbers

Applicability: 500 GT and above, international voyages. HKC entry into force: 26 June 2025. India: Recycling of Ships Act, 2019.
Regulatory References
Hong Kong Convention, 2009, Art. 3Applies to ships of 500 GT and above on international voyages; in force 26 Jun 2025
Recycling of Ships Act, 2019 (India)National implementing legislation
MEPC.379(80), as amended by MEPC.405(83)IHM guidelines and Hazardous Materials list
SOLAS/MARPOL cross-referenceIHM survey linked to statutory certification renewal
CE Oral Tip: Simon wants the exact entry-into-force date, the 500 GT applicability threshold, and India's domestic instrument name. State all three precisely, then connect to Alang's upgraded status as evidence of implementation, not just ratification.
CE Relevance

Before decommissioning, the CE oversees the final IHM update confirming onboard hazardous materials (asbestos, PCBs, ODS) match documentation for the receiving yard's Recycling Plan.

Trap Questions

"Does ratification alone make a yard compliant?" — No; individual yards must separately obtain a Document of Authorization to Conduct Ship Recycling from their competent authority.

Common CE Failures
  • Citing the wrong entry-into-force date for the Convention
  • Not knowing India's specific implementing Act by name
  • Omitting the 500 GT applicability threshold when asked scope of the Convention
Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • 500 GT: HKC applicability threshold, international voyages
  • 26 June 2025: HKC global entry into force
  • 2019: Recycling of Ships Act (India)
Casualty Link

Historical beaching-method incidents at unregulated yards (worker fatalities, unmanaged hazardous waste) were the primary driver behind the Convention's adoption and India's yard upgrade programme.

What is a ship-breaking credit note and how does it work?
Ship Recycling|Commercial
15-Second AnswerA Shipbreaking Credit Note (SCN) is a Government of India financial instrument under the Shipbuilding Financial Assistance Scheme (SBFAS 2.0, notified 24 Sep 2025): a shipowner who recycles a vessel at an HKC-compliant Indian yard receives a tradeable credit worth 40% of the ship's fair scrap value, redeemable against up to 5% of a new vessel's contract price at an Indian shipyard, valid 3 years.
60-Second AnswerThe Shipbreaking Credit Note is not a private commercial settlement instrument — it is a Government of India subsidy mechanism under SBFAS 2.0, administered by the Directorate General of Shipping. When an eligible vessel is recycled at an Indian ship-recycling facility certified Hong Kong Convention-compliant, the owner is issued an SCN worth 40% of the vessel's fair scrap value. The application is made online within three months of the Certificate of Recycling/Demolition Completion. The SCN is transferable/tradeable on the open market, valid for three years, and can be redeemed against up to 5% of the contract price of a new vessel built at an Indian shipyard — the Directorate General of Shipping pays the redeemed value directly to the shipyard, not the owner. It is entirely separate from the BIMCO RECYCLECON, which is the private commercial sale-and-purchase contract for the recycling transaction itself.

Regulatory/Policy Context

The Shipbreaking Credit Note (SCN), also referred to as the Ship Recycling Credit Note (SbCN), is a Government of India financial incentive under the Shipbuilding Financial Assistance Scheme (SBFAS 2.0), notified by the Ministry of Ports, Shipping & Waterways with guidelines effective 24 September 2025. It is not a commercial price-adjustment instrument between shipowner and recycling yard.

How It Works

  • Eligibility: the vessel must be recycled at an Indian ship-recycling facility certified Hong Kong Convention (HKC)-compliant.
  • Value: owner receives a credit equal to 40% of the vessel's fair scrap value.
  • Application: submitted online to the Competent Authority (Directorate General of Shipping) within 3 months of the Certificate of Recycling/Demolition Completion issued by the State Maritime Board/State Government.
  • Redemption: usable against up to 5% of the fair price of a new vessel built at an Indian shipyard; DG Shipping pays the redeemed value directly to the shipyard, not the owner.
  • Transferability/validity: the SCN is tradeable/transferable on the open market and valid for 3 years from issue, after which it lapses.

Distinguishing from Commercial Sale Documents

The SCN must not be confused with the BIMCO RECYCLECON, which is the private commercial sale-and-purchase contract governing the recycling transaction (price, delivery, condition) between owner/cash buyer and yard. The SCN is a government subsidy layered on top of, not part of, that commercial sale.

Key Numbers

Credit value: 40% of fair scrap value. Redemption cap: 5% of new-vessel fair price. Validity: 3 years. Application window: 3 months from Recycling/Demolition Completion Certificate. Scheme effective: 24 September 2025 (SBFAS 2.0).
Regulatory References

Directorate General of Shipping (DGS)Competent Authority for SCN issuance and redemption
Hong Kong Convention, 2009Compliance certification required of the recycling facility for SCN eligibility

CE Oral Tip: Simon is testing whether candidates conflate a government subsidy with a commercial settlement document. Lead with "government financial incentive under SBFAS 2.0," state the 40% scrap-value / 5% redemption-cap figures, and explicitly separate the SCN from BIMCO RECYCLECON.
CE Relevance

The CE does not administer the SCN, but the ship's technical/scrap-value documentation (machinery inventory, IHM) that the CE helps compile feeds the fair-scrap-value assessment underlying the 40% credit calculation.

Trap Questions

"Is the Shipbreaking Credit Note paid to the shipowner in cash?" — No; it is a tradeable credit note redeemed against a new-ship contract, and when redeemed, DG Shipping pays the value directly to the shipyard building the new vessel, not to the owner.

Common CE Failures
  • Confusing the SCN (government subsidy under SBFAS 2.0) with a private commercial price-adjustment note between owner and recycling yard — an immediate oral-exam failure point
  • Not knowing the 40% scrap-value figure or the 5% new-build redemption cap
  • Conflating the SCN with the BIMCO RECYCLECON commercial sale contract
Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • 40%: credit value as a fraction of fair scrap value
  • 5%: redemption cap against new-vessel fair price
  • 3 years: SCN validity from issue
  • 3 months: application window from Recycling/Demolition Completion Certificate
Casualty Link

The scheme is designed to steer owners away from non-compliant beaching-yard recycling by making HKC-certified Indian yards financially attractive, addressing the worker-safety and pollution record that drove the Hong Kong Convention's adoption.

Examiner Chain (Kochi MMD)

Simon: what SBFAS 2.0 is → SCN eligibility (HKC-compliant Indian yard) → 40%/5% figures → distinction from BIMCO RECYCLECON.

On My Vessel (Maersk Container)

Maersk, as a foreign-flag operator, would only realise SCN value if a fleet asset is recycled at an HKC-compliant Indian yard and the company later contracts a newbuild at an Indian shipyard, or sells the tradeable note on the open market; the Chief Engineer's role remains limited to technical handover documentation, not the financial instrument itself.
