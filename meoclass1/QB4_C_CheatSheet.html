ISMSURVEY
15-Second Answer
GISIS is the IMO’s Global Integrated Shipping Information System. It is a centralized, web-based platform used by Member States, Recognized Organizations, and authorized maritime personnel to report, share, and verify critical statutory data. This includes ship particulars, casualty histories, port state control deficiencies, and pollution incidents, acting as a single point of truth to maintain transparency across international shipping.

IACSSURVEY
15-Second Answer
The RO Code is a mandatory IMO framework under SOLAS Chapter XI-1 that governs the delegation of statutory authority from Flag States to Recognised Organisations (Classification Societies). It sets strict criteria for technical competence, governance, and quality management systems. Class is required because Flag States lack the massive technical workforce necessary to globally survey and verify complex vessel structures and machinery.

15-Second Answer
Under ISM Code Section 5, the Master holds absolute overriding authority on board to make any decision regarding safety and environmental protection. The Master&#x27;s Review is an independent, periodic evaluation of the vessel&#x27;s Safety Management System (SMS) to ensure its practical effectiveness and initiate formal technical changes via the company&#x27;s Designated Person Ashore (DPA).

SURVEYIACSSOLAS
15-Second Answer
Goal-Based Standards (GBS) are a tier-structured regulatory framework adopted by the IMO under SOLAS Chapter II-1. Unlike traditional prescriptive rules that mandate how to build a ship, GBS defines the safety goals and functional requirements that a ship’s design must satisfy over its operational lifetime, providing clear design criteria while allowing for technological innovation.
