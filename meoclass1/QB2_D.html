CO2 bottle found empty — how will you plan your watch and what actions will you take?
Fire SafetyISMSOLAS
15-Second Answer: Immediately notify the Master and Chief Officer, and raise an NCR. Assess whether the remaining total CO2 capacity still meets the FSS Code minimum for the protected space. If it falls below the minimum, the system is legally inoperative — I implement compensatory measures (continuous fire watch, suspend hot work) and have the Master notify Class and Flag for a Condition of Class.
60-Second Answer: First, verify the bottle is empty by checking stamped tare weight against actual weight — below 90% of rated charge means discharged. Second, calculate total remaining CO2 volume against the FSS Code requirement (the larger of 40% of machinery space gross volume excluding casing, or 35% including casing). Third, if the threshold is compromised, the ship is technically non-compliant with SOLAS — implement compensatory measures per the SMS: continuous fire watch, halt hot work, restrict high-risk operations. Finally, the Master informs Flag and Class to obtain a dispensation or Condition of Class, while I coordinate sourcing a replacement cylinder at the next port. All actions logged in the engine logbook and SMS deficiency system.

1. Verification & Immediate Assessment

An empty CO2 cylinder is confirmed by weighing, not by visual check, unless the bank is fitted with liquid-level indicators. If the actual weight is less than the stamped tare weight plus 90% of the rated CO2 charge, the bottle is considered discharged and must be replaced.

2. Regulatory Threshold Verification

Cross-reference the remaining CO2 inventory against the vessel's approved fire control plan. Under FSS Code Chapter 5, the fixed CO2 system for machinery spaces must provide a minimum free-gas volume equal to the larger of:

  • 40% of the gross volume of the largest machinery space, excluding the casing above the point where the casing horizontal area is 40% or less of the space's horizontal area; or
  • 35% of the gross volume of the largest machinery space including the casing.

Free CO2 volume factor = 0.56 m³/kg · 85% of total charge must discharge within 2 minutes

Note: these engine-room percentages (40%/35%) are distinct from the 30% figure used for cargo spaces — do not confuse the two on oral.

3. Compensatory Measures (ISM Code)

If the aggregate volume drops below the FSS minimum, the system is classified inoperable. Under ISM Code Element 7 (Shipboard Operations), immediate compensatory measures are enforced: suspend all hot work, isolate hazardous energy sources where practicable, post a dedicated continuous fire watch in the affected space, and brief watchkeepers on heightened awareness.

4. Statutory Notification & Rectification

A vessel no longer meeting SOLAS Chapter II-2 fire-protection requirements cannot legally proceed without authorisation. The Master notifies Class and Flag State immediately; Class typically issues a Condition of Class (CoC) or short-term dispensation permitting passage to the next port for cylinder replacement.

CE Oral Tip (Senthil): Do NOT open with "I will replace the bottle." Senthil is testing ISM response sequencing. Lead with verification → threshold check → compensatory measures → Class/Flag notification → log entry. Know the exact numbers: 90% weight threshold, 40%/35% machinery-space volume, 85% in 2 minutes. Failing to mention Class/Flag notification is treated as an automatic fail.

CE Relevance

If the system is compromised, reassign watchkeeping to cover blind spots normally protected by the fixed system, and scale back engine room operations to conservative parameters to minimise fire risk.

Trap Questions

Q: How do you know it's empty without opening the valve? A: Weighing scale or ultrasonic liquid-level indicator — tare weight is stamped on the cylinder neck.

Q: Can the vessel depart with this deficiency? A: Only with a formal dispensation/Condition of Class from Flag and Class, with compensatory measures actively in place.

Common CE Failures

Saying "just order a new bottle" without checking whether the aggregate drops below the FSS minimum. Omitting Class/Flag notification. Confusing the 30% cargo-space figure with the 40%/35% machinery-space figure.

Numbers to Memorise
  • 90% of rated charge — replacement threshold
  • 40% (excl. casing) or 35% (incl. casing) — machinery space minimum
  • 85% discharge within 2 minutes
  • 0.56 m³/kg — free CO2 volume factor
Casualty Link

Engine room fires where CO2 banks failed to extinguish due to improperly maintained, leaking, or empty cylinders have historically led to total loss of propulsion and abandonment — reinforcing why maintenance verification (MSC.1/Circ.1318) matters as much as the initial installation.

Examiner Chain

Senthil typically asks: How do you verify weight? → What is the FSS formula for the engine room? → What is your SMS procedure if the ship is now non-compliant?

On My Vessel (Maersk A/S)

On a large container ship the CO2 room may serve both the engine room and cargo holds. An empty cylinder might belong to the cargo-hold bank (30% of largest cargo space) rather than the engine-room bank (40%/35%) — identify precisely which protected space is compromised before reporting.

IAMSAR Manual — structure, volumes, and when do you use it?
LSASOLAS
15-Second Answer: IAMSAR is the International Aeronautical and Maritime Search and Rescue Manual, jointly published by IMO and ICAO. Three volumes: I — Organization & Management, II — Mission Co-ordination, III — Mobile Facilities. Volume III is mandatory onboard all SOLAS ships.
60-Second Answer: Volume I is used by governments to set up national SAR systems; Volume II by Rescue Coordination Centres to plan missions; Volume III by ships and aircraft acting as On-Scene Coordinator or participating in SAR. As CE, Volume III is relevant because it's a SOLAS Chapter V carriage requirement. We use it when the vessel receives a distress alert, is directed to assist, or is designated OSC — it contains search patterns, communication procedures and OSC duties that dictate engine readiness, fuel endurance and manoeuvrability during the operation.

1. Structure & Authority

The IAMSAR Manual is a joint publication of the IMO and ICAO, providing a standardised approach for coordinating aviation and maritime SAR operations. It has three volumes: Volume I (Organization & Management), Volume II (Mission Co-ordination), Volume III (Mobile Facilities).

2. Mandatory Carriage — Volume III

Under SOLAS Chapter V, ships are required to carry an up-to-date copy of IAMSAR Volume III. This volume is tailored for ships and aircraft that may be called on to perform search, rescue, or On-Scene Coordinator (OSC) duties, acting as a procedural checklist during a distress scenario.

3. Operational Usage

Volume III is used on receiving a distress relay, or when the vessel must proceed with all speed to assist persons in distress under SOLAS Chapter V Regulation 33. It provides standardised search patterns (expanding square, sector search, parallel sweep) and communication protocols with the MRCC and other responding units.

4. Engine Room & CE Responsibilities

While SAR is conducted from the bridge, the CE supports critically: keeping the main engine on standby for sudden manoeuvres, calculating and reporting fuel endurance for high-speed transits, and ensuring auxiliary power for deck lighting, rescue boat deployment, and continuous communications.

CE Oral Tip (Simon): Simon expects exact terminology. The biggest trap is forgetting the ICAO joint-ownership — IAMSAR is the only major maritime manual shared with an aviation authority. Always connect bridge operations back to the engine room: when IAMSAR is opened, you should already be preparing the plant for immediate manoeuvring and calculating fuel state.

CE Relevance

Provide the bridge with accurate fuel consumption at MCR for transit to the distress location; ensure steering gear redundancy and auxiliary engine readiness for prolonged low-speed manoeuvring during the search pattern.

Trap Questions

Q: Which volume is for the MRCC? A: Volume II (Mission Co-ordination).

Q: Is IAMSAR an IMO-only publication? A: No — jointly published by IMO and ICAO.

Common CE Failures

Correctly stating Volume III is for ships but failing when asked what Volumes I and II cover. Isolating from navigation manuals entirely and failing to explain how SAR impacts engine room operations (fuel, standby, manoeuvring) — this reads as a lack of senior-officer perspective to the examiner.

Numbers to Memorise
  • 3 volumes — I / II / III
  • Volume III — mandatory SOLAS carriage
  • SOLAS Ch V Reg 33 — obligation to render assistance
Casualty Link

Large-scale SAR operations involving both helicopters (ICAO side) and assisting merchant vessels (IMO side) coordinating under a designated OSC illustrate why the joint manual exists and why OSC duties in Volume III matter operationally.

Examiner Chain

Simon frequently asks: What does IAMSAR stand for? → Who publishes it? → What are the 3 volumes? → What is your specific duty down below when the Master announces you are the OSC?

On My Vessel (Maersk A/S)

On a large container vessel, acting as OSC requires coordination given the vessel's windage area and turning circle — the engine room must be prepared for frequent, drastic RPM changes while maintaining power to thousands of reefer containers during search patterns.
