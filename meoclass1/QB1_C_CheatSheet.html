15-Second Answer
Reserve buoyancy is the enclosed, watertight volume of a ship above the waterline. It represents the vessel&#x27;s safety margin against sinking. On the GZ curve, reserve buoyancy dictates the maximum righting lever ( GZ_{max} ), the range of positive stability, and pushes out the Angle of Vanishing Stability (AVS) as it progressively submerges during heeling.

STABILITY
15-Second Answer
Static stability measures the ship&#x27;s ability to resist a constant, steady heeling force, evaluated by the righting lever ( GZ ) at a specific angle. Dynamic stability measures the ship&#x27;s ability to resist sudden, energy-imparting forces like waves or gusty winds, and is quantified by the total area under the GZ curve up to a given angle.

15-Second Answer (Elevator Pitch)**
STABILITY
15-Second Answer
A tender ship has a low metacentric height ( GM ), leading to a small righting moment and a slow, comfortable but potentially dangerous rolling period. A stiff ship has a high GM , producing a large righting moment and a short, violent rolling period. Excessive stiffness creates massive structural stress, risks cargo shifting, and causes severe crew discomfort.

15-Second Answer
A knee (or structural bracket) is a triangular or curved plate used to securely join structural members meeting at an angle. Its primary function is to distribute localized load concentrations, prevent relative movement, and maintain structural continuity across structural joints under dynamic loads.

15-Second Answer
An electronic inclinometer is a high-precision digital instrument that continuously measures, displays, and records a vessel’s dynamic heel and list. Mandated by SOLAS Regulation V/19 , it provides accurate data to the loading computer and generates records for Class quarterly listing reviews to detect structural or stability issues.

If a hole is found in the side hull, immediate actions are to sound the alarm, assess the damage, isolate adjacent spaces, deploy emergency pumps/cement boxes, and report to the Master. A Condition of Class (CoC) is an official directive issued by a Class surveyor requiring a structural defect to be permanently repaired within a strict, specified timeframe.
