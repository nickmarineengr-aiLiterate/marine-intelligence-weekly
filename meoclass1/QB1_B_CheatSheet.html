Conventions & Liability — B Series

21-card visual reference · HNS · PSSA · RO Code · CLC/IOPC · Salvage/SCOPIC · GA · P&I · Subrogation · MLC/RPSL · MACN · PSC · Gender Equality · Compiled by Nixon Antony, 2/E, Maersk A/S

⚖ CONVENTIONS & LIABILITY CORE
Q1
HNS Convention & LLMC Fallback

HNS Convention: Applies to damage from hazardous/noxious substances carried by sea. Two tiers: Tier 1 (Shipowner P&I), Tier 2 (Cargo receiver fund). Not yet in force globally.

Fallback: Where HNS not in force → LLMC 1996 (tonnage-based SDR cap). No second-tier fund. No dedicated chemical spill compensation pool.

Key Numbers
  • Entry: 12 States, 40M GT, 40M tonnes contributing cargo
  • LLMC 1996 limits increased 51% from 8 June 2015
  • HNS applies to IMDG packaged goods on container ships
⚠ TrapHNS Fund cannot pay for Indian waters spill today — 2010 Protocol not in force. Candidates confuse with Bunker Convention 2001 (IS in force).
Regulatory
HNS 2010/Art.7Strict liability framework
LLMC 1996/Art.6Tonnage-based SDR fallback cap
CE Oral Tip"Yes, sir — container ship faces HNS via IMDG. But since HNS isn't in force, fallback is LLMC 1996 or Indian Merchant Shipping Act."
Q2
P&A Manual (MARPOL Annex II)

Purpose: Mandatory ship-specific document for NLS bulk carriers detailing pump/stripping system layout and procedures ensuring statutory discharge compliance.

Issuing Authority: Flag State / Recognised Organisation (RO).

Key Numbers — CORRECTED
  • MARPOL Annex II, Reg 14 — mandatory requirement
  • Pre-2007 ships stripping limit: 75 litres
  • Post-2007 ships stripping limit: 30 litres ← corrected
  • Discharge distance: 12 NM from land
⚠ TrapDo NOT say 75 litres for post-2007 ships. Also: P&A Manual ≠ SOPEP (Annex I) or SMPEP (Annex II emergency plan).
CE Oral Tip — MaerskP&A Manual N/A on container vessel. We carry SOPEP (Annex I) and Garbage Management Plan (Annex V).
Q3
Admiralty Law — Lien, Tort, Statute

Maritime Lien: Proprietary claim attaching to vessel hull in rem. Survives change of ownership. No registration required. Outranks mortgages. Key triggers: salvage, crew wages, collision damage.

Maritime Tort: Civil wrong (negligence) on navigable waters — collisions, dock damage, personal injury. Must prove duty + breach + causation + damage.

Statutory Liability: Strict obligations from IMO conventions (MARPOL, CLC, Bunker Convention) — no negligence proof needed.

⚠ TrapCrew injury in ER = BOTH a tort (negligence) AND statutory claim under MLC Reg 4.3.
Regulatory
MLM CONV 1993Maritime lien priority order
CLC 1992/Art.IIIStrict statutory liability — no negligence needed
Q10
CLC — Scope & Bunker Incident

CLC 1992: Applies ONLY to persistent oil tankers. Strict liability on registered owner. Backed by mandatory P&I insurance.

Bunker spill from Maersk container ship → NOT CLC. Governed by Bunker Convention 2001 — mandatory insurance for all ships >1,000 GT.

Key Distinction
  • CLC = persistent oil tanker spills (cargo oil)
  • Bunker Conv 2001 = bunker fuel spills from any ship >1,000 GT
  • Neither = Annex I MARPOL (that's operational discharge limits, not civil liability)
⚠ Examiner Trap"Your Maersk ship spills 500T HFO. Which convention?" → Bunker Convention 2001, NOT CLC. Saying CLC = instant failure.
Q11
Salvage & SCOPIC Clause

Salvage Convention 1989: Art.13 = "No Cure, No Pay" (reward from salved value). Art.14 = Special Compensation for environmental protection even without success.

SCOPIC: Optional LOF clause replacing Art.14. Invoked by salvor at any time. Guarantees tariff-based payment + 25% flat bonus — even if vessel sinks — funded by P&I Club (not H&M).

Key Numbers
  • 25% — non-negotiable SCOPIC profit uplift
  • Art.13 awards → H&M underwriters pay
  • SCOPIC awards → P&I Club pays
  • LOF = Lloyd's Open Form (SCOPIC is an addendum)
⚠ TrapSCOPIC is NOT an IMO convention — it is a voluntary commercial contract clause created by P&I Clubs, shipowners, and salvors.
CE Oral Tip"Sir, SCOPIC is funded entirely by P&I Club — it covers environmental protection expenditure (third-party liability). Traditional salvage is paid by H&M underwriters."
Q12
CLC & IOPC Fund — Two-Tier System

Tier 1 — CLC 1992: Strict liability on shipowner via P&I; tonnage-based SDR cap.

Tier 2 — Fund Convention 1992: IOPC Fund steps in when: spill exceeds CLC cap, owner insolvent, or owner exempt. Funded by oil cargo receivers >150,000T/yr.

Tier 3 — Supplementary Protocol 2003: Optional; raises total ceiling to 750M SDR.

Key Numbers
  • Tier 1+2 combined max: 203 million SDR
  • Tier 3 total cap: 750 million SDR
  • Levy trigger: >150,000 tonnes contributing oil/yr
  • IOPC Fund payer: oil cargo receivers (NOT IMO, NOT flag states)
⚠ TrapCandidates say IMO or Flag States fund the IOPC Pool. WRONG. Oil cargo receivers (refineries, importers) fund it via mandatory contributions.
Q13
P&I Club vs H&M Insurance

H&M: For-profit corporate insurer. Fixed premium. First-party loss — covers own hull & machinery damage. Capped at hull valuation.

P&I Club: Non-profit mutual cooperative owned by shipowners. Advance calls + supplementary calls. Third-party liability — pollution, crew injury, cargo claims, FFO, wreck removal. Up to ~$1B for oil pollution.

Key Numbers
  • International Group: 12 clubs, ~90% of global fleet
  • Oil pollution pool limit: $1 Billion/incident
  • Running Down Clause: H&M covers 3/4 collision liability; P&I covers 1/4
CE Memory RuleMain engine bearing wipes → H&M claim. OWS fails, sheen overboard → P&I claim. Clear distinction every time.
Q14
General Average — York-Antwerp Rules

GA Act (Rule A): 4 elements — Common Danger + Voluntary Act + Reasonable Action + Successful Preservation. ALL parties share the extraordinary sacrifice proportionally.

CE Formula: Contribution = Total GA Sacrifice × (Your Saved Value ÷ Total Saved Value)

Onboard Procedure: Freeze logs → Preserve physical evidence → Tank soundings → Hold cargo under maritime lien until GA Bond + GA Guarantee signed.

Key Numbers
  • Governing: York-Antwerp Rules 2016
  • Fire pump extra fuel → GA expenditure, shared by all cargo interests
  • YAR Rule VII: Machinery damage in refloating = GA sacrifice
⚠ TrapIf cargo was poorly lashed, cargo owners can refuse GA contributions citing shipowner's unseaworthiness (actionable fault).
CasualtyMaersk Honam (2018) — ULCV fire, GA declared, cargo receivers had to post bonds before container release.
Q19
Clear Ground (Insurance Context)

Definition: Definitive, legally sustainable evidentiary basis required to: pierce LLMC/CLC limitation funds, deny insurance indemnity, or override carrier immunities. Requires proof of personal intent or reckless misconduct.

Applications:
1. Pierce LLMC cap → prove personal act with intent or reckless knowledge (Art.4)
2. Deny H&M/P&I claim → breach of utmost good faith, owner's privity
3. Cargo claims → failure of due diligence under Hague-Visby Art.III(1)

⚠ Examiner Trap"PSC clear grounds" (deficiency → expanded inspection) vs "insurance clear ground" (judicial evidence standard to break limitation or deny claims) — entirely different contexts. Candidates confuse them constantly.
Regulatory
MIA 1906/S.55Insurer exempt for willful misconduct
LLMC 1996/Art.4High threshold to break limitation
Q20
Subrogation

Definition (MIA 1906, S.79): After full claim settlement, insurer steps into the legal shoes of the insured and acquires all rights to sue the third-party wrongdoer. Prevents double recovery.

Three rules: (1) Triggers only after full payout. (2) Insurer gets no greater rights than insured — time bars apply. (3) If recovery exceeds payout, excess goes to insured (not insurer).

Real-World CE Example
  • Off-spec bunkers score fuel pumps → H&M pays for repair
  • H&M then subrogate to sue bunker supplier
  • CE's unbroken MARPOL manifold sample chain = key evidence
  • Tag parts + anti-tamper seal + photo + log immediately after incident
⚠ TrapSubrogation ≠ assignment ≠ arbitration. It means "insurer stepping into insured's legal shoes" — precise language matters in oral exam.
🏛 RO, PSC & FLAG STATE
Q4
STSDSS — Security Training

STSDSS = Security Training for Seafarers with Designated Security Duties (STCW Reg VI/6, Manila 2010 Amendments).

Three levels:
SAT (Security Awareness) → ALL seafarers, basic reporting only
STSDSS → designated security roles in SSP (tactical detection, access control, anti-piracy watches)
SSO → management-level, responsible for full vessel security posture

Key Numbers
  • STCW Reg: VI/6, paragraphs 4–6
  • No 5-year refresher required — life-long validity
  • Maersk Alabama (2009) → piracy — engineering space lockouts critical
⚠ TrapWiper with SAT only CANNOT stand anti-piracy watch — needs full STSDSS (designated security duty under SSP).
Q5
Continuous Synopsis Record (CSR)

SOLAS XI-2/Reg.5: Permanent administrative identity record for all ships ≥500 GT. Tracks flag, owner, ISM company, Class/RO, ISSC issuer — unbroken sequential chain from birth to scrapping.

Three forms:
Form 1 — Official record (Flag State only can write here)
Form 2 — Amendment log (Master fills when change occurs, pending Form 1)
Form 3 — Index of amendments (full history)

Key Numbers
  • Mandatory from: 1 July 2004
  • 500 GT lower limit
  • Never delete or remove old forms — chain must be continuous
  • CSR ≠ Official Log Book (OLB) — completely different documents
⚠ TrapFlag changes mid-ocean → old CSR sheets remain, Master fills Form 2, new flag issues next Form 1. Old sheets NEVER removed.
Q6
PSSA vs MARPOL Special Area

PSSA (IMO Res.A.982(24)): Area with unique ecological/scientific value protected via Associated Protective Measures (APMs) — mandatory routing, ATBAs, reporting systems, discharge restrictions.

MARPOL Special Area: Stricter operational discharge limits only. Does NOT automatically control navigation or routing.

Can overlap: Baltic Sea is BOTH a PSSA AND a MARPOL Special Area (Annex I/V).

Key Numbers
  • 17 PSSAs globally designated
  • Governing resolution: A.982(24)
  • Mediterranean = MARPOL Special Area, NOT a whole-sea PSSA
  • Examples: Great Barrier Reef, Wadden Sea, Baltic Sea, Galapagos
CE Oral Tip"Sir, a PSSA is designated by the IMO MEPC under Resolution A.982(24), protected by APMs like mandatory routing or ATBAs." Distinguish clearly from MARPOL discharge limits.
Q7
Recognised Organisation (RO) Criteria

IMO RO Code (mandatory 1 Jan 2015): Organisation must demonstrate — Technical competence (own class rules), Qualified exclusive surveyors (global coverage), ISO 9001 QMS (regularly audited), Complete financial independence from shipbuilders/owners.

Classification Society vs RO: Class Society = private rules for hull/machinery integrity. Same society + Flag State delegation to issue SOLAS/MARPOL certs = Recognised Organisation. Dual roles, one entity.

Key Numbers
  • RO Code mandatory: 1 January 2015
  • Flag State must audit ROs at least annually
  • Ultimate responsibility always rests with Flag State — never RO
CasualtyErika (1999) / Prestige (2002) breakups → accelerated mandatory RO Code to close class inspection gaps.
Q15
IMO Casualty Investigation Code

SOLAS XI-1/Reg.6 (mandatory 1 Jan 2010): Flag State must conduct structured safety investigation into Very Serious Marine Casualties (total loss, fatality, severe environmental damage).

Non-punitive purpose: Identify root causes to prevent recurrence. Update IMO conventions. Separate completely from criminal or civil liability investigations.

Seafarer rights: Interview in own language, right to legal counsel, medical fitness assessed before interrogation.

CE Action Post-Casualty
  • Preserve VDR data, engine trend files immediately
  • Provide access to: PMS printouts, ORB, fuel sample receipts, safety logs
  • Understand non-punitive context → communicate transparently
  • Stay aware of rights in any parallel criminal inquiry
Q16
Port State Control — Duties & Powers

PSC: Secondary line of defence — enforces SOLAS, MARPOL, STCW, MLC, Load Lines on foreign vessels in national ports. Authority derives from contracting state status.

Progressive Powers:
Initial Inspection → certificates check
Clear Grounds (missing certs, oily bilges, confused crew) → Expanded Inspection
Deficiency notices: Code 16 (14 days), Code 17 (before departure)
Detention: Code 30

Key Numbers
  • Code 30 = detention
  • MoUs: Tokyo, Paris, Indian Ocean (India member)
  • IMO Res.A.1155(32) — PSCO procedures
  • PSC CANNOT inspect charter party/freight invoices
CE Priority Items for PSCClean bilges, functional OWS + recirculation tested, ORB Part I up to date, emergency generator start confirmed, crew drills executed.
👥 SEAFARER WELFARE & COMMERCIAL
Q8
MLC 2006 & RPSL

MLC Reg.1.4: Global standard for seafarer recruitment. No recruitment fees. No blacklists. Financial security against abandonment. SEA signed before joining.

RPSL: National enforcement vehicle issued by DG Shipping, India — proves manning agency compliance with MLC 2006. Shipowners must only use RPSL-licensed agencies (from ratifying countries).

Key Rules
  • Recruitment fees: strictly prohibited (exception: medical cert, seaman book, travel gear)
  • Repatriation cost: borne by company always — never deducted from wages
  • Financial security: 2014 MLC amendments — mandatory abandonment insurance
  • RPSL = Recruitment & Placement Service Licence (DGS India)
CE AlertIf PSC officer interviews engine rating who reveals they paid a bribe to get the contract → immediate MLC non-compliance risk. Regularly brief engine team on MLC rights.
Q9
MACN — Anti-Corruption Network

MACN (2011): Maritime Anti-Corruption Network. Global voluntary business network eliminating bribery, extortion, and facilitation payments in ports. NOT an IMO convention — corporate compliance framework.

Applies to my vessel: Yes — A.P. Møller-Maersk is a founding member of MACN.

Zero-Tolerance Actions
  • Refuse demand. Document it. Notify port agent + company legal.
  • File MACN anonymous non-compliance report.
  • All port technical interactions logged (prevents false ORB claims).
  • Legal anchors: UK Bribery Act 2010 (S.7), US FCPA
⚠ TrapMACN ≠ IMO statutory instrument. It is an independent industry-led NGO with IMO consultative status only.
Q17
Charter Party Types & Vessel Representation

Bareboat: Charterer has full operational/crew control.
Voyage Charter: Owner carries specific cargo A→B, fixed freight.
Time Charter (Maersk): Owner provides crew + technical mgmt; charterer controls ports & pays bunkers.

Vessel Representation: Q88 document guarantees speed/consumption, TEU capacity, reefer plug count, EEXI/CII ratings. Misrepresentation → under-performance claim.

Key CE Points
  • Machinery breakdown → vessel Off-Hire → owner pays all costs, hire stops
  • Under-performance claim → deduction from charter hire
  • Charterer-supplied off-spec fuel → log BDN dispute immediately
  • Charterer directs ports but Master has overriding safety authority
Q18
Tacit Acceptance Procedure

Definition: IMO amendment automatically enters into force on a pre-determined date UNLESS formally blocked by >1/3 of contracting states OR states representing >50% of world gross tonnage.

Process: MSC/MEPC adopts → 10–18 month review window → if blocking threshold not met → automatic global enforcement.

Key Numbers
  • Block threshold (states): >1/3 of contracting governments
  • Block threshold (tonnage): >50% of world gross tonnage
  • Applies to: technical Annexes & Codes only — NOT core Articles
  • Core Articles = explicit acceptance (manual ratification) still required
  • Real example: MARPOL Annex VI 0.50% sulphur cap (IMO 2020)
⚠ TrapCore Articles (SOLAS Art.VIII itself) require Explicit Acceptance. Tacit = Annexes and technical Codes only.
Q21
IMO Gender Equality Initiatives

Strategic alignment: UN SDG 5 — achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.

4 Key IMO Actions:
1. Women in Maritime Programme — WMU (Sweden) + IMLI (Malta) fellowships
2. 8 regional WIMAs (Africa, Asia, LatAm, Caribbean, ME, Pacific)
3. STCW Code amendments — eliminating shipboard harassment during watchkeeping
4. International Day for Women in Maritime — 18 May annually

Key Numbers
  • Women = 1.2% of global seafarer workforce
  • 8 regional WIMAs worldwide
  • Annual recognition: 18 May
  • No mandatory hiring quotas under SOLAS/MARPOL — corporate/national law
CE Answer"As CE I enforce zero-tolerance against harassment, equal PPE in all sizes, merit-based task assignment, and clear reporting channels per Maersk DEI policy."
