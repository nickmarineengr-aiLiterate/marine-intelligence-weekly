Companion to QB4_F.html — Form E, IACS UR/UI, Watchkeeping Principles, ISM Takeover Documents, Cyber Security Basics, LRIT vs AIS. Examiners: Simon, Senthil.

Q1 — Form E

Form E — what is it and what does the examiner expect on this?
What it isRecord of Equipment attached to the Cargo Ship Safety Equipment Certificate (SEC)
CoversLSA (lifeboats, rescue boats, liferafts, lifejackets, immersion suits) + navigational systems (ECDIS, VDR, AIS)
Key regSOLAS Ch I, Reg 12 (Issue of Certificates); SOLAS Ch III (LSA)
ValidityUp to 5 years — matches the SEC
TrapAny equipment change (even upgrading capacity) requires Class approval + Form E amendment — physical inventory must always match the document
Failure pointConfusing Form E with Form C (Construction) or Form R (Radio)
Verbatim line: "Form E is permanently attached to the Cargo Ship Safety Equipment Certificate and must reflect the exact physical reality onboard."

Q2 — IACS UR / UI

UR and UI — what do these acronyms stand for?
UR = Unified RequirementsMinimum technical rules (Class/technical) — members must incorporate into own rules; can be stricter, never less strict
UI = Unified InterpretationsCommon understanding of vague statutory IMO text (e.g. "to the satisfaction of the Administration")
HierarchyFlag State interpretation ALWAYS overrides an IACS UI
IACS membership12 member societies (2026) — RMRS withdrawn Mar 2022, Türk Loydu admitted Nov 2023 restoring count to 12
Key URsUR M (Machinery) · UR Z (Survey/Certification) · UR S-series (bulk carrier longitudinal strength, post-1990s casualties)
Verbatim line: "URs establish minimum technical standards for Class rules; UIs clarify statutory IMO requirements when flag states do not provide specific guidance."

Q3 — Watchkeeping Principles

Principles of watchkeeping — explain.
Key regSTCW Ch VIII, Section A-VIII/2 Part 4-2
5 principlesWatch arrangements · taking over the watch · performing the watch · fitness for duty · environmental protection (MARPOL)
Rest hoursMin 10 hrs / 24 hrs (splittable, one period ≥6 hrs) · min 77 hrs / 7 days
Alcohol limit0.05% BAC (Manila 2010 amendments)
TrapRelieving officer smells of alcohol → refuse handover, inform CE, do not leave ECR. Bilge alarm during handover → relieved officer stays responsible until stabilised.
Failure pointForgetting "protection of the marine environment" as a core principle
Verbatim line: "The relieving engineer must verify machinery status, logbooks, and never take over the watch if the relieving officer is incapacitated."

Q4 — ISM Takeover Documents

Taking over as CE: what documents do you check in relation to ISM?
Key regSOLAS Ch IX; ISM Code Clauses 9, 10, 13
ChecklistSMC + DOC validity · SMS manual revision index · open NCs/CAPA · Near-Miss/Defect reports (last 6 months) · crew familiarisation sign-off · critical equipment PMS status
ValiditySMC 5 years (intermediate audit 2nd–3rd anniversary) · DOC 5 years (annual audits)
TrapDOC expired, SMC still valid → CANNOT sail. SMC validity is tied to DOC validity.
Failure pointMentioning ORB/fuel soundings — these are MARPOL/operational, not ISM document checks. If in doubt, escalate to DPA before signing takeover.
Verbatim line: "I will verify the validity of the SMC and DOC, check the status of all open non-conformities and defect reports, and ensure all engine crew have completed their ISM familiarisation."

Q5 — Cyber Security Basics

Cyber security — basic principles, cross questions. (Foundational version — see QB4_E Q15 for full depth)
Key regISM Code Clause 1.2.2 · IMO Res. MSC.428(98) · MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3
EffectiveFirst annual DOC audit after 1 Jan 2021
5-step frameworkIdentify → Protect (air gaps, USB control) → Detect → Respond → Recover
IT vs OTIT = admin/email networks; OT = engine/navigation control systems — must be air-gapped/segregated
Casualty anchor2017 NotPetya ransomware — hit Maersk's shore IT, accelerated IMO cyber mandates
Failure pointTreating cyber security as IT/deck-only — CE must emphasise OT protection (PMS, ME automation)
Verbatim line: "Cyber risk management is a mandatory part of the SMS under MSC.428(98); as CE, my priority is protecting Operational Technology from malware by strictly controlling USB and service engineer access."

Q6 — LRIT vs AIS

LRIT and AIS — difference, purpose, and basic cross questions.
AIS regSOLAS V/Reg 19 — VHF, ~20-30 NM, open broadcast, continuous update
LRIT regSOLAS V/Reg 19-1 — satellite (Inmarsat-C), global, secure, 6-hr default interval
ApplicabilityLRIT: cargo ships 300 GT and upwards
Data recipientsAIS = anyone with a receiver; LRIT = authorised Flag/Port/Coastal States only
TrapLRIT is NOT trackable on MarineTraffic-type sites (that's AIS). LRIT reg is SOLAS Ch V not Ch XI-2, despite its security purpose.
CE relevanceCE responsible for GMDSS/Inmarsat-C emergency power/UPS backup for LRIT continuity during blackout
Verbatim line: "AIS is a public VHF system for collision avoidance, while LRIT is a secure satellite system for maritime security reporting only to authorised governments."
