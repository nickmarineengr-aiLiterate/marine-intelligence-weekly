🔍 Statutory Surveys & Hull Structural Integrity

2 questions
1
SEQ (Safety Equipment) survey — documents required and how do you prepare for it?
Survey Simon
⏱ 15-Second Answer The Safety Equipment (SEQ) Survey verifies the readiness of a ship's Life-Saving Appliances (LSA), Fire-Fighting Appliances (FFA), and navigational gear as per SOLAS Chapter I. Preparation centers on reviewing the Record of Equipment (Form E) and ensuring all physical equipment perfectly matches the approved LSA and Fire Control plans.
🎯 60-Second Answer Governed by SOLAS Chapter I, Regulation 8, the SEQ survey requires checking the validity of statutory certificates, PMS records, and physically verifying equipment. Documents required include Form E, Fire Control Plan, LSA Plan, Muster List, and servicing certificates for liferafts, BA sets, and fixed fire-extinguishing systems. As Chief Engineer, preparation involves testing the emergency fire pump, ensuring the lifeboat engine starts promptly, verifying fixed CO2/Water Mist system readiness, testing Quick Closing Valves (QCVs), and ensuring emergency generator load tests are up to date. The goal is to prove 100% operational readiness of all safety systems.

Governing Instrument & Principle

The SEQ survey is a statutory survey mandated under SOLAS 1974, Chapter I, Regulation 8 (Surveys of life-saving appliances and other equipment of cargo ships). It ensures that all safety, firefighting, and navigational equipment complies with the International Life-Saving Appliance (LSA) Code and International Code for Fire Safety Systems (FSS Code).

Documents Required

  • Form E: Record of Equipment for Cargo Ship Safety Equipment Certificate
  • Approved Plans: Fire Control Plan and LSA Plan
  • Certificates: Servicing certificates for inflatable liferafts, hydrostatic release units (HRUs), breathing apparatus (BA) sets, and fixed fire-extinguishing systems
  • Records: Updated Muster List, PMS maintenance records for all LSA/FFA, and records of fire/abandon ship drills

Chief Engineer's Preparation (Engine Room Focus)

  • Fire-Fighting Appliances (FFA): Test the emergency fire pump for adequate pressure at the two highest/furthest hydrants. Verify fire main integrity. Ensure fixed CO2/foam/water mist systems are armed, alarms work, and ventilation automatic stops are functional
  • Life-Saving Appliances (LSA): Ensure lifeboat engines start within 2 minutes at ambient temperature, fuel tanks are full, and steering is operational
  • Emergency Power & Shut-offs: Run the emergency generator on load. Test all Quick Closing Valves (QCVs), emergency stop buttons for pumps/fans, and fire dampers for free movement

Examiner Angle (Simon)

Simon assesses whether the candidate clearly separates Deck from Engine responsibilities. He wants to see priority given to machinery under the Chief Engineer's direct jurisdiction — the emergency fire pump, lifeboat engines, and engine room fixed fire-fighting systems — rather than broadly listing deck navigation equipment.

CE Oral Tip (Simon): For Simon at Kochi MMD, mention "Form E" in your very first sentence. The most common failure point is candidates rambling about bridge navigation lights or EPIRBs instead of immediately outlining the CE's responsibility for the Emergency Fire Pump, QCVs, and Lifeboat engines.
🔧 CE Relevance

Routine execution of the Planned Maintenance System (PMS) for safety equipment is crucial. The CE must ensure all test protocols (blowing through CO2 lines, testing water mist nozzles) are logged accurately, as the surveyor cross-references PMS entries with physical equipment condition.

⚠ Trap Questions

"Does the SEQ survey cover the GMDSS and radio equipment?" — No, that falls strictly under the Cargo Ship Safety Radio Certificate (SRRC), governed by SOLAS Ch I, Reg 9.

✗ Common CE Failures
  • Failing to mention testing of Quick Closing Valves (QCVs) and emergency stops for engine room ventilation fans and fuel transfer pumps
🔢 Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • Certificate Validity: maximum 5 years
  • Annual Survey Window: within 3 months before or after each anniversary date
  • Lifeboat Engine Start: must start within 2 minutes at an ambient temperature of 15°C (SOLAS LSA Code)
⚓ Casualty Link

The Norman Atlantic fire (2014) highlighted catastrophic failures in fixed firefighting systems, emergency generator operation, and lifeboat deployment, emphasizing the critical need for rigorous, uncompromised SEQ surveys.

🔗 Examiner Chain (Kochi MMD)

Simon: SEQ Survey → Emergency Fire Pump parameters → fixed CO2 system operation → Quick Closing Valves working principle.

🚢 On My Vessel (Maersk Container)

On Maersk Triple-E class vessels, SEQ preparation heavily involves verifying the Novenco water mist system for the engine room and ensuring all remote pneumatic releases for QCVs in the Fire Control Station are holding pressure and completely leak-free.

Corrosion limits — what are the acceptable corrosion/diminution limits for hull structure?
IACS / Class Simon
⏱ 15-Second Answer Hull corrosion limits are defined by IACS Unified Requirements and Class rules. Generally, a maximum diminution of 20% to 25% of the original as-built thickness is permitted for local framing and plating, while the global hull girder strength (section modulus) must not be reduced by more than 10%.
🎯 60-Second Answer Structural diminution limits dictate the maximum allowable steel wastage before renewal is mandatory. Governed by IACS UR Z7 for dry cargo ships, thickness measurements are taken via UTM. Global limits dictate that the hull girder section modulus cannot experience more than a 10% reduction. Local limits allow for up to 25% reduction in individual plates or stiffeners from their as-built thickness. When wastage exceeds 75% of the allowable margin, it is legally termed "Substantial Corrosion," which triggers mandatory annual thickness measurements for those specific areas. Any steel exceeding the absolute diminution limits must be cropped and renewed.

Governing Instrument & Principle

Corrosion limits are regulated under the International Association of Classification Societies (IACS) Unified Requirements (specifically UR Z7 for general cargo/container ships) and individual Classification Society Rules (e.g., ABS, DNV, Lloyd's Register).

Key Diminution Limits

  • Global Hull Girder Strength: total cross-sectional area and section modulus of the hull girder must not be reduced by more than 10%
  • Local Diminution (Plating & Stiffeners): localized reduction in thickness generally acceptable up to 20% to 25% of original as-built thickness, depending on location and Class society calculation
  • Substantial Corrosion: defined as a wastage in excess of 75% of allowable margins, but within acceptable limits

Practical Application & Measurement

Diminution is measured using Ultrasonic Thickness Measurement (UTM) by Class-approved service providers, typically during Special Surveys (every 5 years) or Intermediate Surveys. If a plate exceeds the 25% local limit, temporary repairs (like doubler plates) are strictly prohibited for structural hull integrity; the affected area must be permanently repaired by cropping out the wasted steel and inserting a new marine-grade plate with approved full-penetration welding.

Examiner Angle (Simon)

Simon targets the candidate's understanding of structural safety boundaries — a clear, numerical distinction between local structural degradation and global hull collapse risks. He heavily penalizes candidates who confuse the 10% and 25% thresholds.

CE Oral Tip (Simon): For Simon at Kochi MMD, explicitly use the term "Substantial Corrosion" and define it as "75% of allowable wastage." The most common failure point is candidates saying "25% wastage is allowed everywhere," completely ignoring the 10% limit on global hull girder strength.
🔧 CE Relevance

Operationally, the CE must actively monitor the condition of the Impressed Current Cathodic Protection (ICCP) system and the condition of sacrificial anodes in sea chests and ballast tanks to prevent rapid localized diminution.

⚠ Trap Questions

"We found a heavily corroded patch on the engine room tank top. Can we weld a doubler plate over it to pass the survey?" — No. Doubler plates are only acceptable as temporary, emergency repairs. Permanent repairs require cropping the wasted steel and renewing it with full-penetration insert plates.

✗ Common CE Failures
  • Confusing the 25% local limit with the 10% global limit
  • Stating that the Chief Officer is solely responsible for all hull corrosion, neglecting engine room double bottoms and sea chests
🔢 Numbers & Regulations to Memorise
  • Global limit: 10% section modulus reduction
  • Local limit: 25% plate/stiffener diminution
  • Substantial Corrosion: 75% of the allowable margin
⚓ Casualty Link

The sinking of the Erika (1999) and Prestige (2002) due to catastrophic structural failure were directly linked to undetected severe localized corrosion in ballast tanks, leading to the strict IACS Enhanced Survey Programme (ESP) and precise diminution limits.

Simon: Corrosion limits → Definition of Substantial Corrosion → Methods of UTM → Permanent vs. Temporary steel repairs → Welding procedures for hull inserts.

🚢 On My Vessel (Maersk Container)

During dry docks on Maersk vessels, the engine room team closely monitors UTM readings of the main sea chest cooling water boundaries and bilge wells, as these areas are highly susceptible to accelerated galvanic corrosion and pitting.
