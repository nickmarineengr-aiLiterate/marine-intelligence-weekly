QB7_D Cheat Sheet — GHG & Decarbonisation, Companion D

One-line recall table for all 11 questions in QB7_D. Built separately per file-size convention (QB7_D has 11 questions).

Currency flag: Q5 (GFI/Net-Zero Framework) covers a fast-moving regulation — adoption was postponed from Oct 2025 to MEPC 85 (Oct 2026). Re-verify before your oral if sitting after Oct 2026.
QTopicCore FactKey Reg
Q1CO2 Sequestration vs CCSSequestration = permanent geological storage step (aquifer/depleted field/basalt mineralisation); onboard capture = extraction step. Together = CCS lifecycle. CII is TtW only — captured CO2 doesn't currently improve CII.MEPC 80/81; IMO Revised GHG Strategy 2023
Q2Onboard Carbon Capture vs ScrubberOCC removes CO2 from exhaust and liquefies for offload; a SOx scrubber only washes out sulphur oxides — NOT the same system, does not reduce GHG.MEPC 80/81 guidance
Q3Amine SeparationFlue gas contacts amine solvent (e.g. MEA) in absorber column, CO2 chemically binds; solvent regenerated in stripper column with heat, releasing pure CO2 for compression/liquefaction. Energy penalty 10–15%.IPCC CCS Special Report
Q4FLL / WtT vs TtWWell-to-Tank = upstream fuel production footprint; Tank-to-Wake = combustion emissions. FLL = WtT+TtW (Well-to-Wake). Matters because CII is TtW-only, so FLL is needed for genuine lifecycle GHG accounting.MEPC.376(80) LCA Guidelines
Q5GFI / Net-Zero Framework2008 baseline = 93.3 gCO2eq/MJ (NOT 91.16). Two-tier target (Base/Tier 1, Direct Compliance/Tier 2). CURRENT STATUS: approved in principle MEPC 83 (Apr 2025); adoption POSTPONED Oct 2025; now expected MEPC 85 (Oct 2026); entry into force ~2027, reporting from 2028.MARPOL Annex VI amendments (NZF); FuelEU Maritime 2023/1805
Q6Ammonia Fuel TypesGrey = steam methane reforming (fossil, high CO2); Blue = grey + CCS; Green = electrolysis using renewable power (near-zero carbon). Key hazards: acute toxicity, corrosivity, N2O slip risk from imperfect combustion.IGF Code amendments (ammonia); IMO interim guidelines
Q7GHG & GWPMain shipping GHGs: CO2, CH4, N2O + black carbon (non-gas forcer). IMO NZF applies GWP20 (not GWP100) for methane — much higher factor (~82.5) to penalise short-lived climate pollutants.IPCC AR6; MARPOL Annex VI
Q8Methane SlipOccurs mainly in low-pressure dual-fuel (Otto-cycle) engines from unburnt scavenge-port gas; also from tank venting/boil-off. High-pressure (Diesel-cycle, e.g. ME-GI) engines have far lower slip.MEPC methane guidelines; IMO GHG Strategy 2023
Q9IGF Code Key RequirementsMandatory for low-flashpoint fuel ships under SOLAS II-1 Part G; core principle = Single Failure Principle (no single fault causes unsafe/explosive condition); double-walled piping, ESD, hazardous-area classification, STCW V/3 crew training.SOLAS II-1 Reg 56/57; MSC.391(95); STCW V/3
Q10Latest MEPC HighlightsTrack most recent MEPC session outcomes for CE-relevant operational directives — verify against IMO MEPC meeting summaries before oral (fast-changing area).Latest MEPC session summary — verify currency
Q11FLL & CE DutyCE's duty: accurate BDN/LHV data entry, correct fuel-type record keeping, avoiding fuel stability/incompatibility risk when switching between conventional and lifecycle-tracked fuels.MEPC.376(80); BDN documentation requirements
