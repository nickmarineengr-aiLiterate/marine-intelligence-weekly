Q1.Constructive total loss — define and explain the criteria for declaring CTL in marine insurance.
A situation where the subject matter insured (the ship) is not physically destroyed, but is damaged or situated such that an actual total loss appears unavoidable, or the cost of recovery and repair would exceed the insured value. The underwriter treats the claim as a total loss upon a valid abandonment.

Q2.P&I Club — what does the 'I' stand for? Explain.
The 'I' in P&I stands for INDEMNITY, not Insurance. A P&I Club is a mutual protection and indemnity association. The club does not pay third parties directly; instead, it reimburses (indemnifies) the shipowner after the shipowner has paid the claim.

Q3.P&I Club calls — explain how club calls (advance, supplementary, overspill) work.
P&I Clubs fund claims using a mutual contribution system called Calls. Members pay an Advance Call at the start of the policy year based on estimated risk. If total claims exceed these funds, a Supplementary Call is levied. For catastrophic claims exceeding club pools, an Overspill Call is charged across all International Group members.

Q4.Oil tanker liabilities — what are the specific liabilities of a CE on an oil tanker? Which conventions apply?
Civil liability for oil pollution rests strictly on the shipowner under CLC 1992 and the Fund Convention. However, a Chief Engineer faces severe personal criminal liability for MARPOL Annex I violations, operational negligence, or falsifying the Oil Record Book (ORB).

Q5.Insurance principles — list all principles of marine insurance and explain each.
The six foundational principles of marine insurance are: Utmost Good Faith (uberrimae fidei), Insurable Interest, Indemnity, Subrogation, Contribution, and Proximate Cause.
