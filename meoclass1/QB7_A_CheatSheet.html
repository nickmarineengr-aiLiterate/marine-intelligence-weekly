GHG, CII, SEEMP, Carbon Capture & EU Regulations

Pre-oral cram aid — Kochi MMD · Primary Examiners: Nair · Rajappan · June 2026

Q1 · SEEMP Part III — Why Added & Contents
Why SEEMP Part III was added
  • EEXI = one-time technical design cert (shaft power limits)
  • CII = annual operational rating — needs a live management tool
  • SEEMP Part III = that mandatory operational management plan
  • Adopted by MEPC.328(76), in force 1 Jan 2023
  • Applies to all ships ≥5,000 GT, international voyages
Mandatory Contents (5 elements)
  • Methodology for attained CII calculation (IMO DCS data)
  • Required annual CII target values (2023–2030)
  • Operational reduction measures (speed opt, weather routing, hull clean)
  • Self-evaluation and review mechanism
  • Corrective Action Plan (when triggered)
Corrective Action Plan Triggers
  • E rating in any single year → immediate CAP required
  • D rating for 3 consecutive years → CAP required
  • Single D rating alone = no immediate CAP obligation
  • No valid SoC → port state detention + barred from international trading
Nair — CE Oral Tip"What happens with an E rating?" State: "E in any single year, or D for three consecutive years, triggers a mandatory Corrective Action Plan." Missing the "three consecutive years" = fail.
⚠ Common Trap"D rating in any single year" requires CAP — WRONG. It requires three consecutive D ratings.
Q2 · Onboard Carbon Capture (OCCS) & Carbon Sequestration
OCCS Process — Amine Scrubbing
  • Exhaust cooled to 40–50°C → enters absorber column
  • MEA (monoethanolamine) solvent binds CO₂ in absorber
  • Rich solvent → stripper column; auxiliary boiler steam 120–140°C breaks bonds
  • Pure CO₂ gas released; lean amine recycled back
  • CO₂ compressed to 15–20 bar, chilled to −30 to −50°C → liquid CO₂
  • Stored in vacuum-insulated Type-C cryogenic tanks onboard
Key Engineering Numbers
  • 3.1 t CO₂ per tonne of HFO burned (mass ratio)
  • >80% target capture efficiency
  • 10–15% auxiliary fuel consumption penalty
  • Boiler steam energy = critical for amine regeneration
Carbon Sequestration — 3 Pathways
  • Depleted oil/gas reservoirs — cap rock traps CO₂ indefinitely
  • Deep saline aquifers — CO₂ dissolves into brine, sinks
  • Mineral carbonation in basalt — CO₂ → solid carbonate (limestone) within years
Rajappan — CE Oral TipMention "auxiliary boiler steam regenerates the lean amine" AND "3.1 tonnes CO₂ per tonne of fuel burned." These are the two differentiating details Rajappan probes.
⚠ Common TrapConfusing OCCS (CO₂ removal — GHG) with SOₓ EGC scrubbers (SO₂ removal — sulphur). Entirely different chemistry and regulatory drivers.
Q3 · EU MRV · EU ETS · FuelEU Maritime · IMO CII Cross-Linkage
EU MRV (Regulation 2015/757)
  • Monitors & verifies fuel consumption and CO₂ on EU port voyages
  • Applies: ships >5,000 GT, voyages to/from/between EEA ports
  • Output: Document of Compliance (DoC) must be carried onboard
  • Data feeds directly into EU ETS allowance calculations
EU ETS (Directive 2003/87/EC)
  • In force for shipping from 1 January 2024
  • Buy and surrender EUAs: 1 EUA = 1 tonne CO₂eq
  • 2024: 40% of verified emissions
  • 2025: 70% of verified emissions
  • 2026+: 100% of verified emissions
  • Non-EU leg (one EU port): 50% EUA coverage
FuelEU Maritime (Regulation 2023/1805)
  • In force 1 January 2025
  • Limits Well-to-Wake Greenhouse Gas Intensity of fuel (91.16 gCO2eq/MJ 2020 baseline — separate from IMO's "GFI" metric, 93.3 gCO2eq/MJ 2008 baseline)
  • Reduction vs 2020 baseline: 2% (2025) → 6% (2030) → 14.5% (2035) → 31% (2040) → 62% (2045) → 80% (2050)
EU Triad vs IMO CII — Key Differences
  • MRV: Data collection → ETS: Carbon tax → FuelEU: Fuel GFI limit
  • CII = global A–E rating, no direct financial penalty
  • EU ETS = regional financial obligation regardless of CII rating
  • FuelEU = WtW (lifecycle) fuel intensity vs CII = TtW (operational) efficiency
  • An A CII rating does NOT exempt from ETS obligations
Rajappan — CE Oral Tip"EU MRV collects the data. EU ETS applies the carbon tax. FuelEU Maritime mandates the fuel intensity limit." Memorise this one-line structure. Then add: "CII gives you a grade; EU ETS gives you a financial bill."
⚠ Common Trap"A CII A-rating exempts you from buying EUAs" — WRONG. CII and EU ETS are separate parallel frameworks with no cross-exemption.
