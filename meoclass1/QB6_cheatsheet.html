🎛️ PID Controller & Ziegler-Nichols Tuning

PID — Three Terms
P
Present error
Fast response, leaves offset
Hunting? ↓ P
I
Past error (accumulated)
Eliminates offset; risk = windup
Drift? speed up I
D
Future error (rate of change)
Brakes overshoot; omit in slow thermal
Overshoot? ↑ D
Onboard Applications
Boiler drum level3-element PID loop
Cargo temp controlThermal (no D term)
Steering autopilotAll 3 terms active
Fuel viscosityPI only
IG pressureP or PI
Ziegler-Nichols Method (Steps)
  1. Stable baseline in manual
  2. Switch to auto; set I = 0, D = 0
  3. Increase P gain → test small setpoint changes
  4. Find Ku (Ultimate Gain — sustained oscillation)
  5. Measure Tu (period of oscillation, seconds)
  6. Apply formulae:
Kp = 0.6 × Ku
Ti = Tu ÷ 2
Td = Tu ÷ 8
Target: quarter-amplitude decay
3-Element Controller (Q15)
Element 1Steam drum water level (trim)
Element 2Steam outflow rate (feed-forward)
Element 3Feedwater inflow rate (feed-forward)
Problem solvedSwell & shrink (false level signals)
Used onSteam LNG carriers, WHRS boilers

🔩 VIT — Variable Injection Timing

PurposeAdvance injection at part-load to maintain Pmax
BenefitOptimises thermal efficiency → lowers SFOC
Active range~40–85% MCR
ImplementationMechanical rack or electronic solenoid timing
Why OFF below ~40% MCR
↓ TC boost (insufficient scavenge air)
→ ↓ Compression temperature
→ ↑ Ignition delay (fuel accumulates)
→ Sudden ignition = KNOCK / DETONATION
→ Pmax exceedance → structural damage
Root causeLack of TC boost air pressure
Rajappan: Lead with "insufficient turbocharger boost" — then the consequence chain. Don't just say "the manual says 40%."
