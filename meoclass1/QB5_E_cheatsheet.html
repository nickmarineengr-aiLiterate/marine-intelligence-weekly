⚡ QB5_E Cheat Sheet

Human Element, Management & Compliance — 7 questions · Kochi MMD, 29 June 2026 · Examiners: Paul, Simon, Senthil

Q1 — Maslow's Hierarchy

5 tiers: Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-Actualisation. Progression + Deficit principles. Sketch the pyramid if asked. Bottom two tiers = MLC/STCW rest-hour mandates; top tier = ISM Element 6 training. Pair with Herzberg (hygiene vs true motivators) for extra credit. Trap: don't answer "motivate with better food/rest" — those are hygiene baselines, not motivators.

Q2 — Hazard vs Risk

Risk = Likelihood × Severity. Hazard = fixed source; risk = variable exposure. Give 3 examples (fuel leak/fire, enclosed space/asphyxiation, working aloft/fall). Know the Hierarchy of Controls: Elimination → Substitution → Engineering → Administrative → PPE (ALARP). Trap: using the terms interchangeably = instant fail flag with Simon.

Q3 — Code of Conduct

NOT a standalone IMO convention — sits in company SMS, backed by MLC 2006 (Std A2.1 SEA terms, Std A5.1.5 complaint procedure) and STCW A-VIII/2 (watchkeeping conduct). BAC limits: STCW max 0.05%, company SMS typically 0.025% or zero-tolerance. Complaint path: onboard → Master → DPA → Flag State → PSC. Trap: calling it a standalone IMO convention.

Q4 — MACN

Maritime Anti-Corruption Network, founded 2011 (Maersk is a founding member), 150+ members. 4 mechanisms: anonymous port reporting, Port Corruption Index, government engagement ("Bridging the Gap"), crew training toolkits. Legal backing: UK Bribery Act 2010 (no facilitation-payment exemption), US FCPA, UNCAC. Trap: never say "small gifts are fine in some ports" — instant fail.

Q5 — Day of the Seafarer

25 June, established by 2010 Manila STCW Conference. 2026 theme: "Carrying world trade. Carrying the risks" — seafarers transiting conflict zones (Strait of Hormuz, Red Sea, Black Sea); IMO coordinated evacuation of ~11,000 stranded seafarers from the Persian Gulf. Do NOT confuse with World Maritime Day 2026–27 theme ("From Policy to Practice: Powering Maritime Excellence"). Verify the theme close to your exam date — it changes yearly.

Q6 — Training Junior Engineers

4-stage OJT cycle: Demonstrate → Co-execute → Supervise → Assess/Log. Structure: Assess → Plan → Execute → Document. Name the TRB and your role as an STCW A-I/6 certified assessor. 4 training phases map to STCW Tables A-III/1 (OOW) and A-III/2 (Management). Trap: vague "mentoring" answers without the structured cycle.

Q7 — CE Takeover: 3 Requirements

State the 3 pillars immediately: (1) Technical State — running hours, spares, defects, fuel/lube soundings; (2) Documentation Status — SMC/DOC/IOPP validity, Conditions of Class, ORB Part I; (3) Personnel Status — STCW certs, familiarisation, TRB progress. Handover window: 24–48 hrs. Trap: signing the Takeover Statement without independently verifying ORB/soundings = you inherit legal liability for pre-existing discrepancies.

Common Thread

This cluster tests leadership/management maturity, not just regulation recall. Simon and Senthil want structured frameworks (3 pillars, 4 stages, 5 tiers) stated up front, then the detail — not a rambling list. Always name the underlying regulatory anchor (MLC/STCW/ISM Element) even in "soft skills" questions.
